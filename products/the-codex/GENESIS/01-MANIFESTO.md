# 01 — MANIFESTO
### What Ash & Archive believes, and what The Codex therefore is

---

## I. The true product

The Codex does not sell character sheets, dice rollers, or spell lists. Those are commodities; a dozen apps have them and none of them matter.

**The product is transformation:**

> Beginner → Competent → Confident → Expressive → Immersive → Master Storyteller

Every feature in this specification exists to accelerate one person's movement along that ladder. A feature that stores information but does not move the user along the ladder is inventory, not product. A feature that moves the user along the ladder is core, even if it looks small.

This has a hard corollary that shapes everything: **the app must produce evidence of growth.** A gym you attend without ever getting stronger is a scam. Most creative software is that scam — it holds your stuff and calls holding "helping." The Codex is architected so that every session of real play generates a record (the Ledger), every record generates diagnosis (the Academy reads the Ledger), and every diagnosis generates deliberate practice (prescriptions). The loop closes. Growth becomes measurable, visible, and — because it is real — addictive in the only honorable way: **addictive through truth.**

## II. Where the philosophy comes from

Ash & Archive did not have to invent a philosophy of storytelling mastery. Its founder already wrote one — a complete operating system for legendary tabletop play, developed over years of practice: the Caliber Charter, the Toy Method, canon governance, Truth-as-Oxygen, pressure clocks, cognitive-load discipline, the at-the-table Loop, the after-action review.

Most software companies bolt a "philosophy" onto a product after the fact. Ash & Archive does the opposite: **the methodology is the product ontology.** The Codex's data model, its AI's constitution, its pedagogy, and its ceremonies are direct translations of a working master-practitioner's system. This is the moat. Anyone can clone a UI. Nobody can clone a decade of craft doctrine — especially when the doctrine is load-bearing in the architecture, not painted on the marketing site.

The name states the architecture:

- **Ash** — the forge. Fire, creation, the live moment, the session burning in real time. Mutable, fast, forgiving. Everything at the Table is written in ash.
- **Archive** — the canon. The bound record, accumulated wisdom, the world's memory. Versioned, trustworthy, permanent. Only what survives the fire is bound in ink.

Every future Ash & Archive product will be a different room built over the same Archive. The Codex is the first room.

## III. The Ten Laws

These are the commissioning brief's ten principles, ratified as law and given their enforcement mechanism. A principle without an enforcement mechanism is a poster; these are load-bearing.

**Law 1 — Reduce cognitive load. Never increase it.**
*Enforcement:* cognitive load is a first-class budget in the product, inherited from the methodology's hard caps (≤7 active moving parts per segment, ≤5 new proper nouns per session, ≤4 visible clocks). The Table literally refuses to show more than the budget allows — the page composes itself down, never up. When a designer wants to add something to a folio, something else must leave. "The page is full" is a valid and final design ruling.

**Law 2 — Every interaction should feel inevitable.**
*Enforcement:* the interaction grammar (Chapter 02) has five verbs, not fifty. Any proposed interaction must be expressible as Turn, Unfold, Inscribe, Kindle, or Bind. If it can't be, either the grammar grows (a governance event, logged and defended) or the interaction is wrong.

**Law 3 — Every screen should teach.**
*Enforcement:* every folio carries a pedagogical layer — not tooltips, but *structure that transmits craft*. The combat folio teaches action economy by making it spatial. The DM's folio teaches the Loop by being shaped like the Loop. The Binding teaches reflection by being a ritual you can't skip mindlessly. A screen that teaches nothing is redesigned until it does.

**Law 4 — Everything should feel handcrafted. Nothing should feel generated.**
*Enforcement:* the provenance ink system. AI output is *visually pencil* until a human binds it to ink. Nothing AI-made can silently pose as human-made, ever, anywhere in the ecosystem. Also: no infinite feeds, no auto-generated filler content, no "here are 20 NPCs" dumps. The Dramaturg proposes few things, well.

**Law 5 — Speed during play. Depth during preparation. Reflection after play.**
*Enforcement:* this is the whole architecture. The three Stances (Table / Desk / Ledger) are not modes of one interface; they are three different relationships to time, each with its own performance budget: the Table answers in ≤80ms and ≤2 gestures; the Desk may be slow, deep, and branching; the Ledger is deliberately paced — a ceremony, not a form.

**Law 6 — Beauty is functionality. Visual hierarchy is gameplay.**
*Enforcement:* the Ledger System (Chapter 03) assigns *meaning* to every visual register: gold means actionable-now, rubrication means a rule of reality has changed, ink-weight means canon status, margin position means advisory. There is no decorative color. A reviewer can point at any pixel and ask "what does this mean?" and there must be an answer.

**Law 7 — The application should disappear. The player should remain immersed.**
*Enforcement:* at the Table, the book turns its own pages. Game state drives navigation; the user navigates only to *overrule* the book. Chrome is absent — no top bars, no tab bars, no hamburger. The folio is the entire viewport.

**Law 8 — The AI should feel like an invisible dramaturg. Never an intrusive chatbot.**
*Enforcement:* constitutional. The Dramaturg has no chat window at the Table. It may only write marginalia — short, pencil-rendered, contextual, capped in number per folio. It never interrupts, never modalizes, never speaks first at the Table. Its full constitution is Chapter 07.

**Law 9 — Information should appear precisely when needed. Never before. Never after.**
*Enforcement:* the Entry graph plus temporal stance make this computable. What appears on a folio is a *function of game state* (whose turn, what conditions, which clock just ticked, which Toy is on stage), not a function of what category the information belongs to. Restrained is the default; the full record always exists one Unfold away.

**Law 10 — The Codex should feel less like software and more like possessing an ancient living artifact.**
*Enforcement:* the object model of the entire product is a book — folios, spreads, marginalia, rubrication, binding, colophons — executed with modern interaction physics. Ceremony is budgeted motion (880ms) reserved for consequential moments only: a death save, a binding, a level attained. Everything else is fast. Sacredness comes from *restraint plus occasion*, never from ornament density.

## IV. The stance on genre

The Codex ships as the definitive D&D 5e / 2024 instrument, because depth beats breadth and its founder's mastery is there. But nothing in the architecture knows what a "hit point" is at the foundation layer. The Entry graph, the Stances, the Dramaturg, the Academy, and the Ledger System are system-agnostic; 5e is the first **Rite set** (rules module) installed. This is not speculative generality — it is the same discipline that keeps the Archive able to host Campaign Studio, World Builder, and every future Wing without schema violence.

## V. What the Codex refuses to be

- **Not a virtual tabletop.** It does not render maps with tokens, does not replace the table. It is the instrument *in the hand of one person at* a table (physical or virtual).
- **Not a content marketplace.** No feeds, no store shelf as home screen, no engagement loops. Retention comes from growth and from a world that remembers, or it doesn't deserve retention.
- **Not a note-taking app with a fantasy skin.** Entries are not notes; they are typed, status-bearing, consequence-linked records. The difference is the entire product.
- **Not a DM-replacement.** The Dramaturg is forbidden (constitutionally, Chapter 07) from authoring outcomes, writing scenes unbidden, or replacing human judgment. It sharpens; it never steers.
- **Not gritty-dark fantasy wallpaper.** The register is scholarly, quiet, warm-dark, and precise. Forbidden aesthetics stand as ruled in the style bible: bright SaaS minimalism, neon cyberpunk, cute mobile-game, glassmorphism, generic dark-fantasy clichés, and the visual language of D&D Beyond / Roll20 / Foundry.

## VI. The oath of the spec

Every chapter that follows obeys the review standard set in the commission: each major decision is defended as if before a panel of Apple HIG designers, IDEO researchers, cognitive psychologists, senior game designers, and veteran DMs — with the user problem, the reasoning, the failures of common software, the rejected alternatives, the superiority argument, the ecosystem scaling, and the honest tradeoffs. Where a decision is preference rather than reasoning, the spec says so out loud.
