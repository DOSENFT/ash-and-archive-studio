# 05 — THE DESK
### The depth stance: preparation as craft, not data entry

The Desk is where time is cheap and depth is the point. Its physics are the opposite of the Table's: branching instead of composed, exhaustive instead of staged, slow ceremony welcome. But it obeys the same grammar (five verbs), the same materials, and one governing conviction inherited from the methodology:

> **Prep like a Toymaker, not an Architect.** The Desk manufactures modular parts that act and react — never scripts that shatter on contact with players.

The Desk has seven rooms. `v2: the Table Covenant was added by the council pass — its absence in v1 was ruled a serious omission.`

---

## I. The Worldshelf

Worlds and campaigns as **bound volumes on a shelf** — the only "home screen" in the product, and it is a bookshelf, not a dashboard. Each volume shows its spine: name, era, session count, canon health (a quiet mark if contradictions await), and the ribbon — where you left off. Opening a volume opens the world's Desk. A player's shelf holds their characters' volumes; a DM's holds worlds; most people hold both.

No stats, no graphs, no "activity feed" on the shelf. You do not open a Moleskine to a dashboard.

## II. The Forge — Beings and characters

Character creation, rebuilt around a conviction V0 half-discovered: **a character is not a sheet plus a backstory — a character is a playable instrument**, and forging one is the first act of training.

The Forge is a **guided sequence of folios** (not a wizard with progress bars — a chapter being written):

1. **The Frame** — race/class/level mechanics, fast and assisted; the Rite set (5e) supplies structure so data entry is selection, not transcription.
2. **The Person** — the Toy Card questions, asked of your own character: what do they *want*, how do they *try to get it*, what is their *active problem*, what would they never do? (The methodology's insight, inverted onto PCs: a PC built as a Toy is playable from session one.)
3. **The Mask** — voice (the 13-slider Voice Forge, kept), mannerisms, first dialogue lines into the bank. The Forge asks you to *say one line aloud* before it lets the Mask bind — the first Rep, disguised as creation.
4. **The Wound** — backstory as *consequence links*, not prose: key memories with emotional cores, relationships as edges to Being Entries, unresolved threads as UNKNOWN Entries with discovery payoffs. A backstory that generates play, structurally guaranteed.
5. **The Binding** — the character binds to ink in ceremony register; the volume gains its spine.

Pencil assistance is available at every step (the Builder voice proposes; you ratify), and the provenance ink means a character forged in ten minutes with heavy pencil help *looks* like what it is until the player makes it theirs — a gentle, honest pressure toward authorship.

Monsters and NPCs forge through the same room with the DM's shortcuts: the **Instant NPC drill** (name → goal → method → two performance hooks → 30 seconds of voice) is both the fast path and, deliberately, Academy Drill A. Creating game material *is* rehearsing.

## III. The Toybox

The parts bin: Toys, Clocks, and Truths as first-class manufactured objects, each authored through its methodology template (Toy Card fields; 4-step clock frames; the Lever Test for truths — the Desk **refuses to bind a Truth that unlocks nothing**, with the refusal worded as craft teaching, not validation error: *"a truth that changes nothing is trivia — what does knowing this let someone do?"*).

Truths author with their ≥3 delivery vectors (rumor/person · document/object · environmental sign) per the path-independence doctrine; the Desk shows vector coverage as three small marks so a truth with one fragile path is visible at a glance.

The Toybox ships with the methodology's libraries pre-stocked as **templates in pencil**: the eight Pressure Clock archetypes, portable NPC types, interrupt patterns. Templates are pencil until adapted and bound — stock parts, honestly marked as stock until made yours.

## IV. The Charter Room — canon governance

The Archive's court. Here live:

- **The Canon Ledger view**: every Ruling, every LOCKED/PROVISIONAL/UNKNOWN Entry, definitions, aliases, dependencies, patch history. Filterable by the four canon layers (immutable gravity / structural / dynamic / local).
- **The Contradiction Bench**: when the Archivist voice detects conflict (at a Binding or on demand), it is docketed here with the methodology's protocol embedded: classification (naming conflict? drift? timeline collision?), three patch options (minimal / clean / story-patch — the last turning the contradiction into an in-world phenomenon with discernment tells), and a Bind to lock the chosen patch. Canon repair as a five-minute craft act instead of a lurking dread.
- **The Readiness Gate**: the methodology's Ready-to-Populate checklist (gravity truths, power lattice, constraints map, faith/magic contract, ≥12 toys, ≥10 truths, ≥3 testable unknowns) rendered as a *gate instrument* — each domain a folio strip filling toward PASS. The Gate governs the Dramaturg's own behavior (Chapter 07): campaign-scaffolding requests against an un-PASSed world are constitutionally refused, and the Gate shows exactly the smallest next-build set instead. The refusal is the product's spine: it will not help you build a campaign on sand.

### Decision Dossier: making governance a room

**Problem:** canon drift is invisible until it breaks trust at the table, and no tool on the market even has the concept. **Reasoning:** what you cannot see, you cannot maintain; governance needs a *place* with instruments, not a policy in a doc. **Common failure:** wikis accumulate contradictions silently forever; nothing in Notion tells you two pages disagree. **Rejected:** (1) fully automatic canon repair by AI — constitutionally forbidden (silent invention is the named cardinal sin); (2) validation-on-write everywhere — makes creation feel like filling in taxes; deliberation belongs at Bindings, not mid-flow; (3) no governance (trust the human) — the human already failed at scale; that's why the methodology exists. **Superiority:** drift becomes visible, docketed, and repairable in minutes, with the repair options themselves teaching the craft. **Ecosystem:** the Charter Room is the trust layer every future Wing depends on (a Lore Engine over drifted canon is a lie generator). **Tradeoff:** governance UI can intimidate casual users — mitigated by it staying dormant until a world grows enough to need it (the room's door appears when there's something to govern).

## IV-b. The Table Covenant — session zero, held by the instrument `v2`

Modern tables run on explicit safety agreements — lines and veils, content consent, an always-available stop signal — and v1 had none of it. The Covenant room repairs that in the product's own register:

- **A structured conversation, not a form:** content lines (never in play) and veils (off-screen only), death and betrayal consent, tone agreements — captured as world-level Rulings during session zero, revisitable any time. The folio is written to be read aloud together.
- **The Veil at the Table** (04 §V): one press by anyone veils the current scene — soft immediate signal to the DM, no reason asked, scene ash auto-marked private, non-disableable. The X-card, translated into the material language of the book.
- **The Archivist keeps the Covenant:** bound ash is checked against the world's lines at every Binding; crossings are flagged in the register of care, not censorship ("this scene touches a veiled subject — confirm it stands as written").
- **Ash consent lives here too:** session scope (what the Quill hears, declared at Table-open), each player's capture level (all / significant moments / manual only — the DM cannot override), and the challenge right (ash about a player is theirs to contest before it can bind — 06 §I).

## V. Session Prep — the Eight Steps

The Lazy-DM-derived eight-step checklist, composed as a **prep worksheet folio**: review the characters (auto-drawn from their Entries — wants, fears, unresolved threads surface *without searching*) → strong start (with the pressure-with-teeth prompt) → 3–6 potential scenes → the session's staged Truths → Places with sensory anchors → NPCs (kindle from Toybox or Instant-Forge) → monsters → rewards-that-change-options.

Each step is 1–3 lines, timeboxed by design — the folio literally fits a 15-minute prep, and the layout *is* the timebox (Law 1 applied to prep anxiety). Output: a **Session Entry in waiting** — everything staged for one-gesture kindling at the Table. Prep connects to play with zero re-finding: what you staged is what the Hidden folio holds.

## VI. The Academy thread at the Desk

Not a room — a presence. The Desk surfaces the current **prescription** (Chapter 06) as a single quiet line on the shelf's ribbon: *"before Thursday: two Consequence Ladder reps — last session stalled twice on faction fallout."* Drills run at the Desk in five-minute folios. The full curriculum shelf, progress chronicle, and diagnosis live in Chapter 06 with the Ledger, because training is prescribed by reflection, not by browsing.

## VII. Search, the drawer, and what the Desk is not

- **Search** exists only here (a Desk instrument, ⌘K, over the whole Archive with Entry-kind and canon-status facets). The Table never needs it by design; if a Table moment sends someone searching, that folio failed and gets redesigned.
- **Settings** is a drawer, not a destination: Rite sets, AI providers and the Dramaturg's Table-silence toggle, sound palette, plain-page mode, data export (the whole Archive as human-readable files — the user's world is the user's, Chapter 08).
- The Desk is not a wiki. Every room above produces *deployable parts* — the Deployability Test (find and field anything in ≤30 seconds) is the Desk's acceptance bar, and the Toybox/Prep/Hidden-folio chain is how it's met.
