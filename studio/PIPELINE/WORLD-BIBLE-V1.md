## §1 — Compass and light plot

- **North is up** on the plan. The building's long axis runs north–south.
- **The fixed hour** (SH1 §1.2, unchanged): one canonical hour, forever — the last
  hour of a winter dusk. **All daylight enters from the WEST**, low and ember-colored.
  East walls never glow; west windows always do. Shadows fall east, long and low.
- Interior key light is always the room's named work-light (lanternlight one-source
  logic, unchanged). Daylight is the *second* voice, never the key — except the
  Approach, which is dusk's alone.
- The Undercroft has no daylight, ever. The Rotunda's oculus shows dusk sky, too dim
  to key.

**The walk (the one chain):** moor → Gatehouse → Great Hall → (west door) Foundry →
(oak stair, up) Loft → (north door) Bestiary Gallery → (processional stair, down,
turning east) Long Gallery, walked south-to-north → (mid-gallery iron gate, spiral
stair down) Undercroft, traversed north → (crypt stair, up) Resonance Chapel → (east
door) Drill Hall → (south arch) Chart Room → (south door) Vestry Rotunda → (west door)
the Garth, open air → (east door) Scriptorium. Finale at the lectern.

Every arrow is an aperture that must be **visible in the preceding leg's final frame**
(SH4 §1.3). Districts are 2–3 legs each under LOCK-V1 (≈3–5 m per leg).

## §4 — District bibles

*Format per district: architecture · apertures (destinations!) · light · focal object &

### 0 — THE APPROACH (the moor and the Gatehouse)
**Architecture.** Black heath under the last dusk. The Archive's south face: rain-dark
ashlar, one great timber double-door under the Gatehouse arch, lantern niches lit.
**Apertures.** The Gatehouse doors → the vaulted passage (G-001) → the Great Hall.
**Light.** Dusk's ember on the west-facing stone edges; the niches' flame warm against
the cold last light. The one frontal composition permitted (Approach exemption stands).
**Focal / inspection.** The doors: iron strapwork over oak — inspect strap oxidation
gradient and the oak's split, weathered grain.
**Details.** Wheel-ruts in the track · boot-worn threshold stone, dished · a mounted
lantern bracket, empty · moss in the north joints only · rain-streak mineral staining ·
door ring polished bright by hands amid black iron.
**Motion.** Heather moving in wind · lantern flames breathing in their niches · low
cloud drifting east · a settling of dust from the arch as the doors stand open.
**Camera.** Long steady approach at walking height across the moor; through the doors
without pause; the passage's vault compressing overhead.
**Copy.** *ASH & ARCHIVE — Where DMs are forged.* One archive. Every campaign you will
ever run.

### 1 — THE GREAT HALL (orientation)
**Architecture.** Double-height hall; hammer-beam roof lost in warm dark; flagstone
floor dished along the walking lines; the Loft balustrade above-west (G-002).
**Apertures.** South: the Gatehouse passage (behind). West: the Foundry door — firelight
already flickering under it. North: the Long Gallery's far arch, dark. East: the
cloister door, closed (Ledger: leads to the garth walk).
**Light.** One great iron candle-ring low over the hall's heart; west clerestory ember.
**Focal / inspection.** The candle-ring: wax stalactites over blackened iron —
inspect wax layering and chain-link wear.
**Details.** A porter's table with an open daybook (blank pages — no-text law), quill
laid across · wall pegs with oil cloaks · an unlit brazier · stacked crates sealed in
blind-stamped wax · the floor's centuries of foot-polish reading as light.
**Motion.** The candle-ring's slow pendulous drift on its chain · flame breathing ·
dust motes falling through the west light · a cloak stirring at its hem in the door
draft.
**Camera.** Enter from the passage; a slow anticipatory turn (≤30°) toward the Foundry
door as the copy lands; drift toward the firelight.
**Copy.** *THE STUDIO.* Nine instruments, one bench. Everything you make here is yours,
bound and kept.

### 2 — WORLD BUILDER — The Foundry
**Architecture.** West wing, ground. Squat stone piers, soot-blacked vault; the great
hearth-forge mid-east wall; the oak stair on the north wall rising east (G-003).
**Apertures.** East: the Great Hall door (behind). North: the oak stair up to the Loft
— its half-landing visible.
**Light.** The forge fire is the key: breathing orange on every surface. West high
windows carry the dusk's second voice.
**Focal / inspection.** The anvil: crowned with a thousand strike-marks, edges rounded
by decades — inspect strike-mark topology and horn burnish. Second: a cooling world-
sphere casting in its sand mold.
**Details.** Tongs racked by size, handles hand-darkened · quench trough, surface
steaming · sand-casting beds with half-buried molds · iron filings drifted along the
bench grooves · charcoal scuttle, spilled · smith's leather apron on its peg, creased ·
chalk layout lines on the slate marking-table (geometry, never letters) · rolled survey
charts in the rack (SH1 accretion lineage).
**Motion.** The fire's breathing and its ember-fall · steam curling off the quench
trough · heat-shimmer above the coals · a chain hoist swaying minutely · filings
skittering when the draft crosses the bench · bellows settling as if just released.
**Camera.** Lateral track along the work line (industrial grammar), the fire wrapping
the lens warm; then the anticipatory settle toward the stair.
**Copy.** *WORLD BUILDER.* Continents, pantheons, economies — struck while hot, kept
forever. Your world is not notes. It is a place.

### 3 — MAP ATELIER — The Cartographer's Loft
**Architecture.** Directly above the Foundry; low king-post roof, plaster between dark
timbers; the balustrade overlooking the Great Hall to the east (G-002).
**Apertures.** South: the oak stair (behind/below). North: the Bestiary Gallery door,
standing open, cases glinting beyond. East: the balustrade overlook (not traversed;
Ledger-recorded).
**Light.** West dormers raking low ember light flat across the drafting tables; two
work-candles with paper shades.
**Focal / inspection.** The master drafting table: a half-finished coastline in iron-
gall ink, weighted flat — inspect vellum tooth and ink-pool sheen. Second: the brass
pantograph, fingerprint-dulled at its handles.
**Details.** Hanging maps drying on lines · pinned expedition notes (marks and route-
lines, no letters) · compasses, dividers, rules in a worn tray · a paint-stone with
ground malachite (the moss-green breath, lawful) · ink bottles ringed with dried tide-
lines · a magnifying lens on an articulated arm · map-weights of river stone · shaved
pencil curls swept to the table's edge.
**Camera.** Ascend the oak stair with the tread (SH4 §4.2), rise into the loft; push in
close over the master table (craft grammar), ease back; settle north.
**Motion.** Drying maps swaying gently on their lines · the candle shades' light
breathing on the ceiling · paper corners lifting in the draft · dust in the dormer
light · the pantograph arm trembling almost imperceptibly with the building.
**Copy.** *MAP ATELIER.* Draw the world you struck below. Every map remembers every
revision — nothing you chart is ever lost.

### 4 — CREATURE ARCHIVE — The Bestiary Gallery
**Architecture.** First-floor gallery running north; specimen cases both walls;
skeletal armatures on plinths; things suspended from the roof trusses.
**Apertures.** South: the Loft door (behind). North: the processional stone stair down,
turning east into the Long Gallery — its first steps visible in the north doorway.
**Light.** West clerestory slits; a lantern (PROP-ONE) at each third case; glass
glinting between darknesses.
**Focal / inspection.** A great horned skull under a case-dome — inspect bone
porosity and the brass case-fittings' oxidation. Second: a wired wing armature.
**Details.** Jarred specimens in amber fluid · labeled drawers labeled only in blind-
stamped sigils · a taxidermist's bench, tools fanned, wax-and-wire work half-done ·
loose feathers on the floor by the bench · a coiled measuring tape · claw and tooth
trays sorted by size · a suspended sea-thing's articulated skeleton overhead.
**Motion.** The suspended skeleton turning very slowly on its chain · fluid in the
jars catching the lantern as it settles · a feather scooting an inch in the draft ·
tiny insects crossing a slit of west light · flame breathing in the case-glass
reflections.
**Camera.** Slow half-orbit (≤30°) around the horned skull's case; then the long
anticipatory drift to the north stair.
**Copy.** *CREATURE ARCHIVE.* Every monster a specimen, every statblock a study.
Catalogued once, summoned forever.

### 5 — NARRATIVE TIMELINE — The Long Gallery
**Architecture.** The ground spine, seven bays (G-004); along its east wall a
continuous carved relief — a timeline in pure imagery: battles, crownings, ruins,
no letters ever. The corridor IS the district.
**Apertures.** Entered from the processional stair at its south end. Mid-gallery,
east side: the iron gate and spiral stair down to the Undercroft (G-005). Far north:
the arch toward the Chapel range (not traversed at ground level; Ledger-recorded).
**Light.** Seven bars of west dusk across the floor, one per bay; a lantern at the
iron gate.
**Focal / inspection.** The relief itself — inspect chisel-mark texture and the
polish where ten thousand hands have touched one carved figure. Second: the iron
gate's lock, worn bright at the keyhole.
**Details.** The relief's episodes aging as the camera walks (older, more eroded
carving northward) · benches worn hollow · a dropped white glove — no. (no personal
effects that imply presence-now; keep just-left lawful): a folded cloth marker
tucked into the relief at one episode · candle stubs in floor sockets · the seven
floor-bars each crossing a different century's flagstone patching.
**Motion.** Dust falling through each successive light-bar · flame at the gate
breathing · a bat's small shadow crossing the far bar (no figures law covers people;
the world's small animals live) · the cloth marker lifting at its corner.
**Camera.** The processional descent with the stair, turning east; then the long
steady walk north through the bars of light — the purest dolly in the Tour; the copy
beats land bay by bay.
**Copy.** *NARRATIVE TIMELINE.* Ten years of play, one unbroken line. Scrub your
campaign's history the way you just scrubbed this hall.

### 6 — RELIC VAULT — The Undercroft
**Architecture.** Beneath the gallery; low groin vaults on stout piers; floor of
laid brick; niches barred with iron; strongboxes on stone shelves.
**Apertures.** South: the spiral stair (behind, G-005). North: the crypt stair up,
lamplight falling down it from the Chapel.
**Light.** No daylight, ever (§1). Two lanterns (PROP-ONE) and a candle-branch; the
deepest obsidian shadows in the Tour — floor detail must survive (SH2 §1.2).
**Focal / inspection.** A reliquary chest: iron-bound, triple-locked, lid-wear
showing centuries of opening — inspect lock-plate burnish and the leather strap's
compression cracking. Second: a wax-sealed scroll bundle.
**Details.** Seals in pigeonholes (Charter lineage) · a ledger stand, book closed,
clasped · dust sheets over the far shelves · a scale with its weights · dripstone
glistening on one pier · a rolled carpet of banners · tally-sticks bundled with cord.
**Motion.** Water dripping into a stone basin, rings spreading · candle flames
guttering slightly in the crypt draft (lawful here: air moves underground) · a chain
settling against a strongbox · the drip's echo-timed glisten running down the pier.
**Camera.** The spiral descent with the tread; a heavy, weighted stop at the
reliquary (the one full stop in the Tour — stops have weight); then north toward the
lamplight on the crypt stair.
**Copy.** *RELIC VAULT.* Every item, every artifact, every session's loot — sealed,
signed, and never lost to a wiped table or a dead notebook.

### 7 — SOUND HALL — The Resonance Chapel
**Architecture.** Tall and narrow; organ pipes filling the north wall; bells hung in
the tower dark above; pendulum weights on long wires down the aisle.
**Apertures.** South aisle: the crypt stair (behind, G-005). East: the Drill Hall
door, ajar, sand-colored light beyond.
**Light.** The west rose window, ember-dim and high; candle-branches at the choir;
brass pipes carrying every flame in long vertical reflections.
**Focal / inspection.** The smallest organ pipe rank — inspect solder seams and
finger-polish on the stop knobs. Second: a bell's rim, strike-worn to brightness.
**Details.** A music stand holding a page of neume-like marks (no letters) · bellows
levers with rope grips · a tuning hammer left on the bench · wax pooled at the choir
rail · felt dampers hung in rows · a swallow's nest high in the tower dark.
**Motion.** The pendulum weights oscillating — the district's signature · a bell
rocking almost imperceptibly, remembering being rung · candle flames leaning together
when the bellows breathe · a glass lamp-shade **vibrating faintly** with the
building's deep settling (the order's "glass vibrating from distant footsteps",
lawfully unexplained) · dust drifting in the rose window's beam.
**Camera.** Rise from the crypt stair into the vertical; a slow crane-up feel *with
the aisle's rising sightline* (never above eye height off the floor — the
architecture does the lifting); settle east.
**Copy.** *SOUND HALL.* Scores, ambience, voices — cued to the scene, conducted from
the bench. Your table hears the world before it sees it.

### 8 — ENCOUNTER FORGE — The Drill Hall
**Architecture.** Broad, war-scarred; timber floor patched and scarred by decades of
drill; pells and mannequin armatures (stands and jointed wood — never figures) down
one side; a great sand table mid-room.
**Apertures.** West: the Chapel door (behind). South: the arch to the Chart Room,
map-light glowing.
**Light.** High west windows, the long bars falling across the sand table; two
lanterns at the racks.
**Focal / inspection.** The sand table: a raked battlefield in miniature relief,
flags of blank linen pinned — inspect the rake-lines and a flag's frayed weave.
Second: a pell's hacked shoulder, splinters bright against old dark wood.
**Details.** Weapon racks (worn practice steel, edges rolled) · chalk circles on the
floor boards · a whetstone bench with oil bottle and swarf · stacked wicker shields ·
rope quintain counterweights · a sand rake and hand bellows · scattered wooden
markers by the table's edge.
**Motion.** The quintain's counterweight swinging down to rest · sand trickling from
the table's raised relief where it was just raked · dust hazing in the window bars ·
a rack chain swaying · flag corners breathing.
**Camera.** Enter low along the racks (lateral track); half-orbit the sand table
(≤30°) as the copy peaks; settle south toward the arch.
**Copy.** *ENCOUNTER FORGE.* Build the fight before the night: initiative, terrain,
morale — balanced here, so at the table it just *runs*.

### 9 — CAMPAIGN ENGINE — The Chart Room
**Architecture.** The east wing's heart: one great table bearing the campaign chart;
pigeonhole walls; a brass orrery by the window.
**Apertures.** North: the Drill Hall arch (behind). South: the Vestry Rotunda door,
lamplit.
**Light.** Candles down the table's spine under smoke-darkened reflectors; one west
window on the orrery.
**Focal / inspection.** The campaign chart: routes in pinned red cord across a
painted land, wax seals at the waypoints — inspect cord tension shadows and seal
impressions. Second: the orrery's worn crank and meshing gears.
**Details.** Pigeonholes stuffed with sealed dispatches · a wax-jack and seal press ·
sand-caster for blotting · campaign tokens in a bone tray · a folded field-tent of
maps mid-table · dividers walked across the chart and left standing · candle
reflectors' soot fans on the ceiling.
**Motion.** **The orrery turning** — slow, geared, indifferent (the district's
signature clockwork) · candle flames drawing toward the door when it moves · the red
cord's tiny shiver · smoke curling off a just-snuffed wick at the table's far end ·
sand grains settling in the caster.
**Camera.** Push slowly down the table's spine — the Tour's most inevitable dolly;
the orrery passes close at frame-right, gears legible; settle south.
**Copy.** *CAMPAIGN ENGINE.* Arcs, factions, consequences — the long game, kept in
motion between sessions. The world advances even while the table sleeps.

### 10 — CHARACTER SANCTUM — The Vestry Rotunda
**Architecture.** A domed rotunda ringed with deep alcoves; in each alcove an armor
stand, a costume form, or an empty banner pole — regalia without wearers (ADR-SH2-C
holds absolutely; the people are *implied by their things*, never shown). A dim
oculus at the dome's crown (dusk sky, too faint to key — §1).
**Apertures.** North: the Chart Room door (behind). West: the garth door, standing
open — open air, the sapling, and across the garth the Scriptorium's east door with
candlelight inside (the finale visible before it is reached: anticipation as
architecture).
**Light.** A lantern (PROP-ONE) on a central plinth; each alcove holding its own
small candle; the oculus a deep blue-umber coin overhead (coolest lawful dusk grade,
within band).
**Focal / inspection.** A champion's cuirass on its stand: every dent honest, straps
sweat-darkened, one pauldron newer than the rest (a story in metal) — inspect dent
topology and strap grain. Second: a signet ring on a velvet tray.
**Details.** A tailor's form pinned with a half-made cloak · boots resoled twice by
different hands · a shelf of personal effects in labeled-by-sigil boxes · a mirror
turned to the wall (lawful wit, ≤1 per room) · comb, whetstone, dice of horn in a
belt pouch spilled open · a child-sized wooden sword among the steel.
**Motion.** Candle flames in the alcoves breathing at different rhythms · the pinned
cloak's hem swaying · dust falling through the oculus coin · the banner pole's
pennon lifting at its tip in the garth draft.
**Camera.** A slow reverent half-orbit of the rotunda's center (≤30°), alcoves
passing in candle-rhythm; settle west toward the open garth door.
**Copy.** *CHARACTER SANCTUM.* Every PC and every NPC — sheets, portraits, voices,
secrets — kept like regalia between sessions. People are the campaign. Keep them.

### 10b — THE GARTH (crossing, not a district)

**Apertures.** East: the Vestry door (behind). West: the Scriptorium's east door,

**Light.** The only open sky in the Tour's interior: last dusk above, ember west

**Motion.** The sapling's leaves trembling · drying herbs swaying under the walk ·

**Camera.** Six steady meters of open air — the breath before the finale. No copy

### 11 — THE CODEX — The Scriptorium (finale)
**Architecture.** The heart. Writing benches in ranks; the three west windows over
them (G-007); shelves of bound volumes gold-capped and umber (accretion lineage);
mid-room, the **great lectern**, and upon it the Codex itself — the illuminated
tactical grimoire, open.
**Apertures.** East: the garth door (behind). North: a closed door (Ledger: the
Dramaturg's lodge — silent by law, never opened, its silence kept as canon).
**Light.** The three west windows in full last-ember voice; two candle-branches
flanking the lectern; the Codex's leaf catching both — the brightest lawful surface
in the world (world highlight ceiling still holds; the page's own ink-light belongs
to the page layer, not this render).
**Focal / inspection.** The Codex: leather cover blind-stamped, corners bumped
round, gilt worn to the high spots, silk register ribbons, the open leaf showing
illuminated *imagery* only (no letters — the no-text law survives even here; the
book's geometry and illumination carry it). Inspect: cover grain and gilt wear;
ribbon sheen. Second: the lectern's claw feet, floor-worn.
**Details.** An inkhorn and three quills in graded wear · pounce pot and burnisher ·
a page under a paperweight awaiting binding · the sealing station: wax, taper, press
(the Binding rite's home) · shelf spines gold-capped by the campaigns already bound ·
a reading candle with a paper shade.
**Motion.** The register ribbons stirring · candle flames leaning as the west light
dies a degree · dust in the window bars over the benches · a page corner lifting and
settling, as if the book is breathing.
**Camera.** Through the door, down the bench rank aisle, the slow inevitable push to
the lectern; the Tour's final settle — a held, weighted stop on the open Codex. The
camera never touches it. The user's hand does, at the CTA.
**Copy.** *THE CODEX.* Everything the walk taught, bound in one book. *(CTA: Enter
the Archive — begin your first Volume.)*
