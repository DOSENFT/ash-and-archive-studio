The image is liminal and dreamlike — a scene recalled from childhood or a recurring dream, aching with the sense of a world that existed once and cannot be returned to. **The sharp plane is where the memory lives; the blur is time.** Any use that reads the building or a room as a model remains the named prohibition (§1.1).

### 1.2 The lighting law (minted here — measurable; the "inherited" citation was a ghost, per U-3)
- **The world highlight ceiling:** in any world still, the mean CIE relative luminance (sRGB) of the brightest 1% of pixels ≤ **0.8 × Y(#fff5eb)** — no world pixel region ever reaches the register reserved for the page's display ink. Measured on **each of the six graded outputs** per still, and on the neutral master (A-2, A-4). *(P-5, P-6.)* The 20% margin is 2.5× the ~8% brightness JND — margin, not decoration.
- **One-source logic:** each bay is lit by exactly one work-light — its `{ROOM_KEY_LIGHT}`: the Studio lantern (§5.5) in six bays, the oil lamp in the Academy, **the Forge's fire (two authored states: cold / lit, per SH1's readiness accretion)** — plus at most one window. Light has a reason or is removed. No volumetric shafts, haze, flare, or rim halos. *(U-5, purist D-3.)*
- **Darkness is generous:** deepest shadow at obsidian #141310, never pure black; floor detail survives at the ground value.
- **The warm band, numbered (canon, here — A-4 and SH1 §5.4's ΔE-8 tint derivation both depend on it):** environment hue OKLCH H ∈ [20°, 110°], chroma ≤ 0.09; neutral = chroma < 0.01, permitted only where L < 0.25 and ≤5% of pixels; **plus one breath of moss green (ADR-SH2-F): H ∈ [130°, 150°], C ≤ 0.05, ≤3% of pixels — the garth's living things (the sapling, lichen on stone), never walls or light**; zero pixels outside band + breath. "Violet-dusk" (SH1 §5.4) is realized as the coolest low-chroma dusk grade *within* the band — the name is poetic, the pigment is umber. Never cold-grey: **dawn-grey is the warm band's palest grade, not an exception to it** *(P-7)*. The product's only cool tone remains the pencil grey, and it belongs to the page.
- **Dawn belongs to thresholds:** window light brightens only in the Waking and the Approach.
- **The fixed hour (the shadow law):** every shadow in every still is cast at the **same canonical hour, forever** — shadow angles never vary across poses, assets, or years. The Studio's shadows never move; the workshop stands outside time, per the sundial (SH1 §5.3). Machine-checkable against the master. *(Taste Part 3, signature 3.)*

### 1.3 Composition
- Doorways frame destinations; accretion anchors in view but peripheral — evidence in the corner of the eye, never presented.
- **One aperture per frame:** every composition contains exactly one way through (door, window, or gate), at a working third. The light source sits off-center. *(Signature tic; countable.)*
- No symmetric hero frames — **sole exception:** the Approach still, frontal, once.
- Deep focus everywhere except dedicated Toybox assets (§3) — with the memory-blur clause (§0) as the sanctioned focus treatment *within* deep-focus compositions: the plane of sharpness holds the frame's heart; the melt at near edge and far dark is optical, never depicting the room as small.

### 1.4 The one scale-reveal (constitutional) *(P-1 applied)*
Only the **Approach's held still** — a separate, once-per-install, reclaimable asset (SH1 §3.2) — may show the Cloister whole and small, lamplit in the dark. Then the camera crosses the gate and the world is full-scale, permanently. **The permanent shelf pose is a different asset and frames the door at human scale** — the lamplit threshold filling the frame, the building never readable as a whole (§5.4). Scale wonder is spent once; the Waking never re-spends it. No other asset, at any register, shows the building from outside or above.

### 1.5 Named prohibitions (any hit fails the asset, regardless of polish)
Floating diorama islands · toy-scaled world or figures (Approach still exempt by name — the one sanctioned smallness) · isometric or orbital views · miniaturizing tilt-shift (the focus instrument per §0/§1.1 is lawful; the model-world read is not) · claymation, origami, papercraft, low-poly, voxel, or any stylization with a name · volumetric god-rays, haze, flare · neon, glass, chrome, plastic · out-of-band hues (§1.2 numbers) · pseudo-text · figures or their traces · symmetric hero shots (save the Approach) · **any frame recognizable as a template, a trend, or default AI output — runnable as A-9's second question: shown cold, "have you seen this image before?"; a yes fails** *(A-8 made testable)*.

## 2. THE PURIST-BRIDGE DOCTRINE (binding; reworded per purist D-1/D-5)
Two honest lists, never conflated:

These are *required dressing* (prop canon §5.5) — the doctrine's claim is only as true as the walls.

The Studio never depicts the user's fiction; SH1's output-shape rule governs art identically. **The bridge test (A-9): shown any still cold, a 20-year paper DM names ≥5 physical craft objects unprompted and reads zero elements as videogame; one such read fails the asset.**

## 3. THE TOYBOX — OBJECT LAW AND CAMERA LAW *(split per P-3)*

**Object law (travels everywhere a toy renders — N-2):**
- Toys are physical craft objects: carved wood, folded card, cast brass, honest wear, a maker's knife-marks, a paint-worn edge.
- **Pieces, not portraits:** a location is a carved token, not a rendered place; a person is a chess knight, not a face. The piece stands for the thing; the imagining stays at the table.
- **Piece provenance (purist condition 1, non-negotiable; P-4b):** pieces come from a **closed, authored catalog — the parts drawer — keyed to schema kind only** (every location draws from *the* location-token family; every being from *the* knight family). **The user's hand assigns piece to entry; the software never selects, shapes, or varies a piece from any property of the entry it stands for.** The hand that picks the piece is the imagining. Audited at A-14.
- **The physicality test (A-1):** could this object sit on a real wooden table as a plausible handmade thing? Fails if it could only exist as a render of fiction, **or if its form derives from any Entry, name, or user-authored content** *(P-4c)*. A physicality litmus, not a period aesthetic: the Studio's language is its own and timeless.

**Camera law (dedicated Toybox assets only — never poses, never seam frames):**
- Proximity, not god's-eye: the craftsman bends close over small things — luthier, watchmaker, miniature painter. Down and in, never up and away; the bench fills the frame; the room behind falls to shallow focus (the sole shallow-focus jurisdiction). Named exemption to §1.1 eye height and A-5, scoped here and closed.
- The Forge's **seated pose** remains deep-focus at eye height with the alcove peripheral — it is a seam-duty frame and obeys §1.1 absolutely.

**The Table staging surface is page jurisdiction (ADR-SH2-D; P-4a):** it renders in Ledger iconography — thin-stroke, hexagonal-frame register — never photographic toy renders. World-layer toy imagery exists only in world stills, which are never on screen while seated (clause 8 bench silence). A folio never gets a texture; a wall never gets a UI gradient.
