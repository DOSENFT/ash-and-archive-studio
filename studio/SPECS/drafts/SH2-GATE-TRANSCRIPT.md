## PART A — THE CANON PROSECUTOR (verbatim)

[Filed 2026-07-14. Constitution stack: canon clause 8 · SPEC-SH1 v1.0 SEALED · Ledger System · GENESIS 09 §II.]

**P-1 · THE SHELF POSE IS A PERMANENT EXTERIOR — SCALE WONDER IS SPENT ON EVERY WORLD-OPEN · CRITICAL.** SH2 §1.4: "Scale wonder is spent once… no subsequent asset, at any register, may show the building from outside or above." As drafted the user sees the building small on every world-open forever, violating SH2's own §1.4 eleven lines earlier. Failed defense: "same asset, so no *subsequent* asset exists" — fails; SH1 declares the Approach asset *reclaimable*; a reclaimable asset cannot be the seam-duty still of a permanent pose. CONVICTED. Amendment: split the framings — shelf pose frames the door at human scale, frontal (sanctioned symmetry survives); the whole-Cloister framing exists only in the Approach's held still, separate, once, reclaimable.

**P-2 · "EIGHT CROPS OF ONE MASTER" BREAKS THE SEALED SEAM ECONOMY · MAJOR.** SH1 §2.2 (sealed): 18 locked poses, frame-identical seams, O(N) library, spiked at ΔE76 0.11. **P-3 · THE TOYBOX CAMERA LAW COLLIDES WITH THE FORGE'S SEAM-DUTY POSE · MAJOR.** N-2 as written ("one law, no per-surface exceptions") makes the Forge seated still — a seam-duty frame with toys in it per SH1 §5.3 — owe the Toybox camera (down-and-in, shallow), breaking §1.1, §1.3, A-5, and the sealed ENTER/EXIT seams. **P-4 · THE TABLE STAGING SURFACE HAS NO JURISDICTION — AND IT IS THE ATLAS'S THIRD DOOR · MAJOR.** (a) Clause 8: seated, the world layer is fully dormant — if the staging surface is world imagery it renders seated (bench-silence breach); if page material, ADR-SH2-A's "a folio never gets a texture" forbids photographic carved-wood renders. ## PART C — THE PURIST ADVOCATE (verbatim)

*I run my table off a recipe box of index cards, two binders with cracked spines, and maps I draw in pencil because pencil admits it might be wrong. I came here to convict. Read the whole thing before you read my verdict, because I did not arrive at it easily.*

**D-1 · HIGH — The bridge doctrine names my objects; the dressing law never renders them.** §2 promises "index cards and card boxes, bound volumes, wax and seals, minis and parts drawers, rolled charts, pressure-clock dials." Now walk the actual dressing: **there is no index card anywhere in this building.** No card box. The doctrine promises my recipe box and the rooms deliver a monastery. The objects that would make me nod — a wooden card box with a worn thumb-notch, dividers with blind-stamped tabs, a fat rubber-banded stack at the back — appear in the *claim about me* and nowhere in the *law about the walls*. That's the definition of flattery. The chart rack made me nod (I own map tubes). The retired dials leaning in a drawer-corner made me nod hard — "retires worn instruments; it does not mount kills" is the truest sentence in either spec. The doorframe marks are the best object in the whole building — that's a home, not a scoreboard; whoever wrote "smudged chalk wear well before any run of marks is countable" has actually stood at a doorframe. But the doctrine's first-listed object is missing from its own world. **Change:** the card box enters the dressing law as named required dressing, with the sundial's specificity.

**D-2 · CRITICAL — The Toybox law holds the line on *what a piece looks like* but is silent on *whose hand shapes it*.** "A location is a carved token, not a rendered place; a person is a chess knight, not a face" — the good law, the line that separates a mini from a cutscene. But the spec never says who chooses the piece. If my entry is a dragon named Vermithrax and the software decides, from anything about that entry, that the piece shall be a beautifully carved dragon — the software just did the imagining. It picked my dragon's face one abstraction level up and called it restraint. A carved dragon chosen by the machine *because my entry is a dragon* is the Atlas wearing a woodcarver's apron. SH1's line covers visual input and output shape on terrain; piece-*selection* provenance falls between the laws. At my table **I** grab the badly-primed owlbear to stand in for the lich because it's the mini I have, and that wrongness is half the joy. The hand that picks the piece is the imagining. **Change (non-negotiable):** "Pieces come from a closed, authored catalog — the parts drawer; the user's hand assigns piece to entry; the software never selects, shapes, or varies a piece from any property of the entry it stands for." Plus its audit row. Without this sentence I convict; with it, this section is the best thing software has ever said to me.

**D-3 · MEDIUM — The style anchor contradicts the lighting law in at least one room.** "One lit candle-lantern as the key light," verbatim in every prompt, versus the Forge whose work-light is constitutionally the fire — which is also a state that changes. Two key lights, or a lie in one of them. **Change:** per-room key-light slot; the Forge's two fire states named.

**D-4 · MEDIUM — There are no dice in this workshop.** No die, no dice tray, anywhere — while SH1's own event vocabulary has two dice event types. Dice are the single most universal physical object of this craft, and they are not user fiction; they are the *instrument*, exactly the class §2 claims to honor. A worn wooden tray with the finish ground off its center passes every law in this spec and needs no accretion channel. Its absence tells me the dressing was derived from the scriptorium register first and my table second. **Change:** dice tray on the Stage bench. If it wants to double as the Stage's wit entry — a single die on the floor under the bench, half-lost the way every real die is eventually lost — I will personally forgive the wax seals.

**D-5 · LOW — The wax seals are the workshop's liturgy, not my craft; the doctrine conflates the two.** No paper DM in twenty years has sealed anything with wax. Within the product's own canon the seals are earned and coherent — but §2 claims them as *mine*, and that's the one place it slips from "your craft honored" toward telling me what my craft is. I snorted. Once. **Change:** two honest lists — objects of the purist's craft, objects of the workshop's own rite. The bridge test only needs the first.

**D-6 · LOW — For the swift-handed user the entire accretion system is a 240ms glimpse — accepted, but name it.** At tier 3 (or "swift from the start," which is what I'd choose) the workshop's decade of evidence exists in drift-cut flashes and threshold beats. I tried to convict this and couldn't — it's how a real room works; I don't stare at my shelf either, but I know it's there. Record the trade: the heaviest asset investment in this product will be experienced almost entirely at saccade speed. SH1-α already hedges correctly. **Change:** none required; a sentence acknowledging the trade would make the record honest.

**REJECTED OBJECTIONS:** (1) "Theme-park monk" — dropped: the register is the product's own pre-existing language carried to the walls; it never puts a candle on my page; the airlock keeps the regimes apart; cosplay is when the costume claims to be you, and this building never claims to be my dining room — except in §2, which is why D-1/D-5 stand and this doesn't. (2) "The candle for a death performs my feelings at me" — I came in certain this was my kill shot, and it's the opposite: no camera, no music, the page holds the grief, and the world's whole answer is a small permanent flame you find later, in passing. I keep my dead PCs' cards rubber-banded at the back of the box. Same gesture. Whoever wrote "the world's response to the largest human moment is its smallest gesture" understands honoring versus performing better than most humans I've played with. Dropped, with respect. (3) "The tint renders my canon" — dropped: caught violating its own line in draft, struck, rebuilt from a ULID and a count, and *the weakened claim is recorded as a deliberate trade*. That's the opposite of software lying to me. (4) "The Binding rite is a cutscene tax" — dropped: ≤2.4s, skippable at any frame, bank-the-fire as an honest no-rite door, permanent demotion "honored without copy that sulks." Weight I can always decline is weight, not tax.
