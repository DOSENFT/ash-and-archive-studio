### PART 3 — TIER 3 vs THE BASELINE: THE CENTRAL ATTACK

The question is not whether 7.68s across ten hours matters as wall-clock (it doesn't). Attention residue is per-event: what matters is whether each of the 32 transits delays the *hand*. So: does the spec guarantee the destination is interactive at ~0ms under a drift-cut?

**It does not. Nowhere.** The evidence:

- §2.4 grants interactivity-before-settle only inside the `landing` state, and `landing` is only entered *by input*: "**Any input** during `flying` (key, click, wheel, gamepad, palette invocation) transitions to `landing`: the flight snaps forward (≤120ms accelerated ramp, G-SH1-4) and the destination is interactive before the landing frame finishes settling."
- The airlock's interactivity clause is likewise landing-scoped: "the folio accepts input under the **landing's** visual tail" (§2.4).
- The drift-cut is explicitly a Passage — "drift-cuts included in 'Passage'" (§2.4) — so it traverses `queued → flying → landing → seated` like any flight. During its `flying` phase, the machine as written gives the keystroke a snap ramp, not the folio.
- §4.2 `seated`: "destination UI focused and interactive *first*; then teardown" — interactive first *relative to teardown*, which says nothing about the flight.

So the mechanically specified tier-3 transit is: up to 120ms `queued` (if the page was animating — and with 280ms State and 520ms Transition motions everywhere per GENESIS 03 §VI, it often is) + a 240ms `flying` crossfade during which the first keystroke buys a ≤120ms snap instead of a character on screen. The hard-cut baseline accepts the keystroke at compose (~80ms folio paint). **Tier 3 as specified is not at-or-below baseline; it is up to ~240ms of blocking seam per transit, ~32 times a day, applied precisely to the most-traveled routes of the most committed users.** The type-ahead replay guarantee (§2.4, "the triggering keystroke is replayed into the destination") preserves *correctness*, not *latency* — your character appears late, every time, forever. That is the definition of a fatigue tax. The spec's own G-SH1-1 gate ("ON may not exceed control at p50") *intends* the right thing, and the Phase 0.5 spike S-1 ("does not raise time-to-first-input versus a hard cut") *intends* it too — but a gate and a spike cannot rescue a state machine that structurally forbids the outcome. If S-1 is honest, it fails the machine as written.

### FINDINGS

**F-1 · CRITICAL — The drift-cut is blocking by construction; tier 3 is not at-or-below the hard-cut baseline.**
Text at fault: §2.4 "**Any input** during `flying` … transitions to `landing`: the flight snaps forward (≤120ms accelerated ramp)" together with §2.4 "(drift-cuts included in 'Passage')" and §2.3 "it costs 240ms."
Ten-hour failure: 32×(up to 120ms input latency + occlusion) on the exact routes a professional travels most; the layer taxes mastery in perpetuity, inverting the canon amendment's own promise ("the hundredth day is faster than the first," §1).
Minimal amendment: add to §2.3 — *"At tier 3 the four-state machine does not apply: the destination folio mounts complete, focused, and interactive at frame 0; the drift-cut is a non-blocking visual overlay above an already-live page; input is delivered directly (no snap, no replay). `queued/flying/landing` exist only for clip flights (tiers 0–2)."*

**F-2 · CRITICAL — G-SH1-1 is measured by an instrument that cannot see the failure.**
Text at fault: §2.8 route_log schema: "`ttfiMs` (**seat** → first meaningful input)" versus G-SH1-1: "time-to-first-meaningful-input **after navigation**."
Ten-hour failure: if ttfiMs starts at *seat*, the entire flight — the only thing being litigated — is excluded from the measurement. A fully blocking 240ms drift-cut logs the same ttfiMs as a hard cut, and G-SH1-1 passes on a build that is strictly slower than the control 32 times a day. This is the canonical "passes the gate, fails the hand" defect.
Minimal amendment: redefine — *"ttfiMs is measured from the navigation input event to the destination's first processed meaningful input, inclusive of queued, flying, and landing time"* — and state in G-SH1-1 that the control arm's clock starts at the identical event.

**F-3 · HIGH — The `queued` snap window taxes every departure from a live page, at every tier, including the palette.**
Text at fault: §2.4 "a Passage holds in `queued` for at most one 120ms snap window, then flies"; §1 "No Passage begins while page motion is in flight"; §2.6 palette "selection lands seated via drift-cut at most" (a drift-cut is a Passage, §2.4).
Ten-hour failure: GENESIS 03 pages breathe constantly (280ms State, 520ms Transition, rubrication bleeds, unfolds). A navigating professional frequently issues navigation *during* those. Every such departure pays up to 120ms before the world even begins — additive to F-1, and it reaches the palette path, whose §2.6 promise is "instant, sovereign." The palette *opens* instantly; its *landing* does not, and the spec never notices the contradiction.
Minimal amendment: *"The queued window may not delay destination interactivity: the outgoing page's snap-to-end and the destination's mount proceed concurrently; queued gates only the WorldStage's first frame, never input delivery. Palette-invoked navigations are additionally exempt from any queued hold."*

**F-4 · HIGH — Per-directed-route familiarity halves the decay rate of every corridor and decays slowest exactly where routes are longest.**
Text at fault: §2.5 "Familiarity `f` = lifetime completed traversals of a **directed** route"; §2.6 back/forward "Reverse route, one tier faster than its familiarity."
Ten-hour failure: Forge→Charter and Charter→Forge are separate counters, so the day's dominant corridor needs 40 traversals — not 20 — before both directions are resident. The back/forward mitigation binds only to the back/forward *input*; a GM clicking the shelf or using ⌘K for the return leg (the normal behavior) gets no credit. Worse, the inversion: my log's 8 cross-cloister transits are spread across 6 distinct directed routes — the 2.2s routes accrue f slowest, so the *longest* flights survive the *longest*. The decay curve is steepest where it matters least.
Minimal amendment: *"A completed traversal of a→b also credits b→a at half weight"* (undirected half-credit), and *"any traversal at ring-distance ≥3 credits both constituent spokes, so cross-cloister familiarity generalizes."*

**F-5 · MEDIUM — Tier boundaries (f=3/8/20) put a professional's entire first week inside the slow tiers.**
Text at fault: §2.5 tier table ("0–2 · full composition, 1.0×" … "≥20 · drift-cut").
Ten-hour failure: day one of my log is 55.8s of world layer; with directed counters, even the hot corridor doesn't reach tier 3 until roughly day three or four, and cross-cloister routes take weeks. The spec's own defense of the user is "the hundredth day is faster than the first" — but the buyer being courted is a professional evaluating the tool in week one, at 1.0×–1.5×. Not fatal (skippability and the pin-to-tier-3 setting exist, §2.5), but the defaults front-load the tax onto the trial period.
Minimal amendment: expose the existing "pin any route to tier 3" as a first-run global choice (*"swift from the start"*), or lower boundaries to f=2/5/12.

**F-6 · MEDIUM — G-SH1-6 (bench silence) is passable by a probe that never hovers, while the spec itself schedules decode work during seated work.**
Text at fault: §4.2 "**preflight** (on navigation *intent* — hover ≥150ms on a room affordance …): decode destination poster still … Budget: ≤ 24MB decode, ≤ 80ms main-thread total" versus G-SH1-6 "GPU/decoder allocation attributable to the world layer during 10 minutes of seated work | Zero."
Ten-hour failure: hover-triggered preflight fires *while seated* — mousing past the shelf during work triggers up to 80ms of main-thread world-layer activity, hundreds of times over ten hours. A 10-minute profiler run that avoids room affordances (rubric probe #4 as written) passes; a real day does not experience silence. Additionally §4.2's teardown ("completes ≤ 500ms after interactivity; never before it") schedules world-layer work in exactly the window where the user's first post-transit inputs land.
Minimal amendment: G-SH1-6 measures *"10 minutes of seated work including ≥30 incidental hovers over navigation affordances; preflight main-thread cost per event ≤ 4ms, all decode off-thread"* and teardown is required to run at idle priority, yielding to input.

**F-7 · MEDIUM — The cross-cloister composition doesn't fit its own ceiling.**
Text at fault: §2.2 clip durations (EXIT ~0.5s, SPOKE ~0.7s, ENTER ~0.6s) vs §2.3 "Cross-cloister (distance 3–4) … 2.2s (ceiling)."
Ten-hour failure: EXIT + SPOKE + SPOKEʳᵉᵛ + ENTER = 0.5+0.7+0.7+0.6 = **2.5s > 2.2s**. Either the clips are secretly rate-adjusted at tier 0 (unstated), or the constitutional ceiling is breached by the launch asset set. An unbuildable table is an unenforceable table.
Minimal amendment: state the reconciliation — e.g. *"cross-cloister routes play at 1.15× base rate at tier 0"* — or re-cut SPOKE to ~0.55s.

**F-8 · LOW — Tier-1 behavior is undefined for garth-origin routes.**
Text at fault: §2.5 "Tier 1 … EXIT elided (start at lintel), 1.5× rate."
Garth→bay routes contain no EXIT (§2.2: spoke + enter); the elision rule has nothing to elide, and my tier-1 arithmetic for class S required an invented convention. Minimal amendment: one sentence — *"routes without an EXIT segment elide the spoke's first half at tier 1."*

**F-9 · LOW — The drift-cut claims residency "inside the State register," contradicting the spec's own C-2 resolution.**
Text at fault: §2.3 "it costs 240ms, inside the State register" vs §0 C-2 "the four registers govern *ink on the page* … The world layer exists only *between* rooms."
The spec's constitutional defense of the 2,000ms flight is that page registers don't apply to the world layer; it cannot then borrow the State register's authority to bless 240ms. Cosmetic, but it is exactly the kind of register-blurring rubric probe #14 exists to catch. Minimal amendment: strike the clause; the drift-cut is justified by G-SH1-1, not by register membership.

**F-10 · MEDIUM — G-SH1-3's thresholds certify flight duration, not experienced speed; a passing build can be slower than hard cuts everywhere.**
Text at fault: G-SH1-3 "tier-3 routes ≤ 300ms; a 30-day-active profile's mean navigation ≤ 400ms" and §2.8 "`flightMs` (planned vs actual)."
Ten-hour failure: 300ms of *blocking* flight passes G-SH1-3 while being 300ms worse than a hard cut per transit; the 400ms mean permits a steady-state that is ~1.7× the asymptote forever. Only G-SH1-1 defends the hand, and F-2 shows its instrument is broken. Two of the three quantitative gates are duration gates when the harm is a latency harm.
Minimal amendment: add to G-SH1-3 — *"and per-route input-inclusive ttfi (per F-2's definition) at tier 3 ≤ hard-cut control + 0ms at p50."*

**REJECTED ATTACKS** (the spec survives; recorded honestly):

- **R-A · Memory-and-decode accumulation over ten hours:** rejected — §4.3's ceilings (0 dormant / 32MB transitional / 120MB flight), 60s unwarm eviction, and §4.2's revoke-on-seat are explicit and G-SH1-6-measurable; nothing compounds.
- **R-B · Visual-noise accumulation (accretion clutter growing all session):** rejected — §5.3 "All counts log-scale past legibility … Nothing displays a number," accretion is glimpsed-in-travel only and never idle-animated (§5.1).
- **R-C · Monotonic no-regression rule:** rejected — "a vacation doesn't reset your hands" (§2.5) is the correct polarity; regression would be the fatigue tax.
- **R-D · Palette *opening* latency:** rejected — §2.6 "Opens instantly over any state including mid-flight" plus rubric #5 (<100ms from every state) is airtight for the open; only the landing leg is compromised (F-3).
- **R-E · The opt-out path as a shame tax:** rejected — §4.7 is full-parity, instant, "no mourning copy," and G-SH1-5 makes >20% opt-out a design failure rather than a user failure. This clause is the best-written escape hatch in the document.

### VERDICT

The geography, the asset economics, the failure ladder, and the ceremony discipline survive this review. What does not survive is the tier-3 promise the commission asked me to test: **as mechanically specified, the resident-tier Studio is not at-or-below a hard-cut baseline — it is up to ~240ms of blocking seam (queued hold + landing snap) per transit, ~32 times per prep day, enforced by a state machine (§2.4) that its own gates (G-SH1-1, S-1) implicitly assume doesn't apply, measured by an instrument (§2.8 ttfiMs) that starts its clock after the harm ends.** F-1 + F-2 + F-3 must be amended before seal; each amendment is one paragraph, and all three are compatible with everything else in the document. The spec's intent is right. Its text, today, permits a build that fails its own taste standard 32 times a day — quietly, at 240ms, which is precisely how fatigue taxes ship.

### PROSECUTION 1 — THE MOTION-LAW LOOPHOLE HUNT

**C-1 · CRITICAL — The Approach is unconstitutional under the draft's own ceiling clause**

Text under prosecution:
- §1: *"**The ceiling is constitutional.** No Passage exceeds 2,200ms and no Rite exceeds 4,000ms, written here, not in configuration. Raising either requires a new signed ADR; no build flag, setting, or asset may exceed them."*
- §2.3: *"First launch | the Approach (§3, crossed) | ~8s, once, skippable"*
- §3.1 crossed ceiling: *"≤ 1.6s, skippable, most decay"*

Derivation attempted: Find a register that legally hosts an ~8-second world beat. There is none. The Approach is classified **crossed** (§2.3, §3.2 enumeration), whose ceiling is 1.6s — exceeded 5×. Read it as Passage: ceiling 2,200ms — exceeded ~3.6×, and the clause explicitly says no **asset** may exceed it; the Approach clip is an asset. Read it as a Rite: ceiling 4,000ms — still exceeded 2×, and §3 says bound is *"two acts, and only two,"* neither of which is first launch. The draft's flagship first-run moment is illegal under the amendment the same document proposes. Worse, this proves the ceiling clause is already being treated as having unwritten exceptions — which is exactly how a decorative-motion advocate will treat it next ("the Approach exceeds the ceiling and shipped, so ceilings admit taste exceptions").

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: add to the ceiling clause — *"One sole exception, named here and closed: the Approach, played at most once per install, ≤8s, skippable by any input, asset reclaimable after. No other exception exists or may be created without a signed ADR."* Alternatively cut the Approach to ≤4s and classify it a Rite; either way the exception must be written into the constitutional text, not left as a contradiction for later lawyers.

**C-2 · HIGH — "Ink on the page" is a jurisdiction gap: on-page non-ink motion answers to no law**

Text under prosecution:
- §0 C-2 / §1 framing: *"the four registers govern* ink on the page *— motion inside a room. The world layer exists only* between *rooms and* at *consequences."*
- GENESIS 03 §I: obsidian is the ground and *"never carries text"* — obsidian is definitionally not ink and not page.
- GENESIS 03 §VI (current law): *"Motion is meaning. Four durations, three easings, and a hard law that nothing else exists."*

Derivation attempted (SUCCESSFUL): GENESIS 03 §VI governs **all motion** — "nothing else exists." The amendment narrows the four registers' jurisdiction to *"ink on the page."* I now render a slow candlelight shimmer on the obsidian ground visible around the seated folio, or a 900ms breathing gradient on the desktop margin rail, or drifting dust motes in the room backdrop behind the page. Defense: (a) not ink, not on the page — so the four registers don't govern it under the amended scope; (b) not Passage, not Rite, not rendered by `<WorldStage>` (which is *"mounted ONLY in flying/rite states,"* §4.1) — so it isn't "world layer" either and the two world ceilings don't bind it; (c) "the bench is silent" speaks of *"the world layer"* being dormant — this motion is room-UI chrome, not world layer. A third, lawless motion category has been created by scoping the old law by **material** (ink) instead of by **layer**. The old law had no such gap because it governed motion, period.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment to C-2/§1: *"The four registers govern **every pixel that moves while a page is seated** — ink, ground, margin, chrome, and rail alike; the world registers govern every pixel that moves while no page is seated. No motion exists outside these six registers."* Scope by layer and time, never by material.

**C-3 · HIGH — The word "idle" undoes the bench-silence clause; the Binding Rite proves the clause is already broken**

Text under prosecution: §1: *"**The bench is silent.** Seated at any instrument, the world layer is fully dormant — zero residency, zero idle motion."*
Versus §3.2 bound: *"the camera lifts **from the bench** and drifts once past the Chronicle's shelf."*

Derivation attempted (SUCCESSFUL): Two-step. First: the Binding Rite runs while the user is seated at the bench — the world layer is manifestly not "fully dormant" seated. So either the apex Rite violates the constitutional clause, or the clause carries an unwritten Rite exception. Second, the qualifier *"zero **idle** motion"*: I now propose the forge fire flaring for 800ms at the bench whenever `binding.sealed {mode:'banked'}` fires. Defense: this is not *idle* motion — it is *responsive* motion, and the clause only bans idle motion. The marked tier's "0ms at act time" blocks this for **marked** acts specifically, but "idle" invites a whole class of "responsive world gestures" at the bench for anything I decline to classify as marked (e.g., a new "noticed" tier). The valve word is doing illegal work.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: *"Seated at any instrument, the world layer is fully dormant — zero residency, zero motion of any kind, idle or responsive — except during a Rite or crossed beat enumerated in §3, which are the sole seated exceptions."* Strike "idle."

**C-4 · MEDIUM — The constitutional airlock binds Passage only; Rites are exempt from it as written**

Text under prosecution: §1: *"**The airlock.** **Passage** and page motion never run concurrently. No ink animates until the page is fully seated and static; no **Passage** begins while page motion is in flight."*

Derivation attempted (SUCCESSFUL): The airlock names Passage twice and Rite never. During the Binding Rite's world exhale (≤2.4s), I animate a margin note blooming onto the page in rubrication-bleed style, simultaneously with the camera drift. Defense: the airlock forbids concurrency between *Passage* and page motion; this is a *Rite*, a distinct register, so the constitutional text permits overlap. Rubric line 15 would catch it ("zero frames where ink animates while the WorldStage is mounted") — but the rubric is a test document, not the constitution, and the amendment is what gets ratified into canon. A canon clause that depends on a QA checklist to mean what it intended is a defect.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: replace both instances — *"No world register (Passage or Rite) and page motion ever run concurrently."*

**C-5 · MEDIUM — "Interactivity is not motion" contradicts its own type-ahead guarantee; the clause opens a micro-motion door**

Text under prosecution:
- §1: *"post-Passage interactivity is not motion — the destination accepts input under the landing's tail."*
- §2.4: *"the triggering keystroke is **replayed into the destination** (type-ahead safe)."*
- §2.4 airlock: *"no ink animates until the WorldStage has unmounted and the page is seated."*
- Rubric 15: *"zero frames where ink animates while the WorldStage is mounted."*

Derivation attempted (SUCCESSFUL, two ways): (1) Internal contradiction: a replayed keystroke necessarily paints a character, moves a caret, draws a focus ring — ink visibly changing while the WorldStage is still mounted (the landing tail is still settling). The spec's own rubric 15 fails the spec's own type-ahead feature. (2) The loophole: if the defense is "input echo doesn't count as motion," then I extend the same defense to any input-triggered feedback — a 120ms micro hover-glow, a pip toggle, a gold brighten — all running under the landing tail, and "interactivity is not motion" becomes "any motion I can attribute to an input is not motion."

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: *"Interactivity is not motion: instantaneous input echo (character paint, caret, focus indication — zero-duration state changes) is permitted under the landing's tail; any **registered** motion (micro or above — anything with a duration and an easing) remains forbidden until the WorldStage unmounts."* Amend rubric 15 to match.

**C-6 · MEDIUM — The Sanctum falls outside the bench-silence law: no seated pose, no "instrument," no defined jurisdiction**

Text under prosecution:
- §1: *"Seated at any **instrument**, the world layer is fully dormant."*
- §2.2: *"8 **seated poses** (inside each bay, at its bench…) · 1 **garth-center pose**"* — the Sanctum has no seated pose.
- §6.1: *"The garth resolving to the Sanctum also settles the recovered Director's-Sanctum pattern into geography instead of a dashboard."*

Derivation attempted (SUCCESSFUL as ambiguity): The Sanctum is a working destination (the director's court, a recovered product pattern — presumably with UI). But the user is never "seated at an instrument" there — the garth has no bench and no seated pose, and the lifecycle's `seated` state maps to seated poses. Defense: at garth-center, bench-silence does not apply by its own words, so the world layer may remain resident and in motion around the Sanctum's working surface — the sundial's shadow could creep, the sapling could sway, at Passage-register durations, indefinitely, because the user is never "seated." Nine destinations, eight covered by the silence law.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: *"'Seated' means occupying any destination pose, bench or not; the garth-center pose is the Sanctum's bench for every purpose of this clause."*

**C-7 · LOW — The demotion rule mints ceremony acts outside the closed map**

Text under prosecution: §3.2: *"A second crossed/bound beat in the same sitting demotes one tier."* Versus rubric 11: *"≤ 12 marked channels, 5 crossed, 2 bound. A single act outside the map fails."*

Derivation attempted (partial): A second Binding in one sitting demotes bound→crossed: a ≤1.6s world beat that is none of the five enumerated crossed thresholds (which are all defined as once-or-rarely life events). Rubric 11's census would count a sixth crossed act, or the auditor must carry an unwritten "demoted acts inherit their parent's legality" rule. No decorative motion escapes through this — the demoted beat is smaller than the original — but the closed-set bookkeeping is formally broken, and closed sets that admit unwritten members stop being closed.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED (bookkeeping).** Minimal amendment: *"A demoted beat counts against its original tier's enumeration, not the tier it demotes into."*

### PROSECUTION 2 — SMUGGLING THE ATLAS THROUGH ACCRETION

**C-8 · HIGH — The Forge map pins are the Atlas seed: per-entry, kind-specific sprites on depicted terrain**

Text under prosecution: §5.3: *"Forge | … **map pins (bound `place` entries)** … | … entry counts"*
Versus GENESIS 09-II: *"The world as an infinite zoomable canvas — map at the top, regions, settlements, dungeon rooms, NPCs as pins."*

Smuggle attempted (SUCCESSFUL): The channel passes §5.1 literally — input is a count. But examine what renders: one pin per bound `place` entry, on a depicted **map**. That is a per-entry spatial representation of exactly the entry kind the Atlas was killed for spatializing. The smuggle's growth path is frictionless: (1) v1, N pins at pre-authored positions — already reads to every user as "my world's places," and the workshop is now lying (a coastal town's pin sits wherever the artist put slot 7); (2) the first bug report reading "my places are in the wrong spots" invites (3) ordering pins by binding order — now each pin **corresponds** to a specific entry with zero user content consumed, a 1:1 entry↔pin mapping that §5.1's provenance audit cannot fail; (4) "just let the pin show the place's name on hover" and the Atlas is home. §5.1 polices *inputs*; this channel's *output shape* (individually enumerable sprites, kind-specific, on terrain) is the Atlas regardless of inputs. Note every other channel renders undifferentiated mass (spines, wear, thickness) — pins are the only channel whose sprites invite pointing at.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: strike "map pins"; replace with non-cartographic surveying evidence (rolled charts accumulating in a rack, log-scaled — mass, not markers). Add to §5.1's line: *"No accretion channel may render individually enumerable sprites of a single entry kind upon a depiction of terrain, regardless of input provenance; accretion renders mass and wear, never markers."*

**C-9 · HIGH — The docket tray renders OPEN work: it violates the draft's own "bound work only" law and is a solicitation meter**

Text under prosecution: §5.3: *"docket tray thickness (**open contradiction cases**)"*; §3.2 marked: *"the Bench's docket **thins**."*
Versus §5.1: *"It renders **bound work only** — never activity, never streaks (C-4)."* And §2.1's own standard: *"Any lodge affordance that draws the eye during travel is an engagement mechanic and a constitutional defect."*

Smuggle attempted (SUCCESSFUL): Open contradiction cases are pending obligations — not bound work, not even work done. A tray whose thickness is your open-item count, glimpsed on every pass of the Charter Room, is a badge count in period costume: it says *come resolve me* exactly the way the lodge is constitutionally forbidden from saying *come see the Dramaturg*. The draft convicts this pattern itself, one room to the west, then ships it in the Charter Room.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: the docket channel renders **resolved** cases — filed case-folders accreting on the Bench's shelf (`charter.resolve` counts, log-scaled); open cases render nothing anywhere in the world layer.

**C-10 · MEDIUM — Drill slate wear is fed by activity, not bound work**

Text under prosecution: §5.3: *"drill slate wear (Rep count, log-scaled) | … `rep.performed`"* versus §5.1: *"It renders **bound work only** — never activity."*

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: feed the slate from bound evidence only (reps cited in bound sessions, or strike the channel — the window-lamp rungs already carry the Academy's accretion).

**C-11 · HIGH — The sundial breaks accretion determinism by construction and fails rubric 13 on day one**

Text under prosecution: §5.3: *"the sundial's shadow set by **session-proximity** … | **shell state**"* versus §5.1: *"It is derived, deterministic state: same event log ⇒ same workshop"* and rubric 13: *"Diff the workshop stills of two profiles with identical event logs: **byte-identical composites**."*

Smuggle attempted (SUCCESSFUL, three convictions in one channel): (1) Determinism: session-proximity is wall-clock-and-schedule shell state; two profiles with identical event logs at different times of week produce different sundial shadows — rubric 13 fails by design, and the draft ships a rubric its own §5.3 cannot pass. (3) Engagement: a shadow that creeps toward "session soon" is an anticipatory, real-time-keyed display — the closest thing in the whole spec to a countdown, i.e., the workshop watching the clock at you.

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: strike session-proximity; the sundial's shadow derives from an event-log fact (e.g., the hour-of-day of the last `binding.sealed`, frozen until the next seal) — or the Sanctum keeps only the sapling. If the recovered Sanctum behavior is wanted anyway, it needs its own signed exception to §5.1 and an explicit carve-out in rubric 13; it cannot ride in silently.

**C-12 · MEDIUM — "Toy shelf filling" is a fill-me meter by its own verb**

**C-13 · MEDIUM — §5.1's "countable archive state" does not constrain the grouping key; counts grouped by user-authored dimensions reconstruct world structure**

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment to §5.1: *"…counts, statuses, seals — aggregated only along schema-defined dimensions (entry kind, canon status, provenance, event type, seal mode) — never grouped, ordered, or positioned by any user-authored value (names, tags, aliases, relationships, dates, or coordinates)."*

### ARITHMETIC & CLASSIFICATION AUDIT — the "54 of 60" claim

Classification contradictions:

**VERDICT: CONVICTED.** Minimal amendment: re-state as *"66 of 68 event types are silent; `binding.sealed` is marked (banked) or bound (full); `binding.ratified` is silent (machine-emitted inside the transaction whose seal already carries the weight — classified here so no act stands outside the map)"*; resolve `clock.ticked{step:4}` and `rep.performed` to marked or strike their channels (per C-10, strike); define the confirmed-death derivation; and file a one-line erratum against SPEC-001's "Sixty types" header, which is independently false in the substrate spec.

### PART 3 — THE INEVITABILITY VERDICT

This spec is closer to inevitable than any shell-layer document has a right to be: the drift-cut's preserved bearing, the decay curve as structure rather than playback trick, the silent lodge, the candle, and the §5.4 paragraph that *records its own weakened claim* are IDEO-honest and Nintendo-economical, and a veteran would feel their absence the way you feel a missing stair.
