# SH3 · GATE 1 TRANSCRIPT — three fresh-context hostile reviewers vs SPEC-SH3 v0.1
*2026-07-16 · Per precedent (SH1 Gate 1). Reviewers dispatched fresh-context (none saw the drafting), each against the sealed canon set + the v0.1 draft. Verdicts reproduced verbatim below; dispositions follow. Result: **42 findings, 42 accepted, 0 rejected** → draft v0.2.*

---

## REVIEWER 1 — THE TEN-HOUR FATIGUE ADVOCATE (verbatim)

### Findings

**F-1 · CRITICAL · The `ready` promise has no deadline: a slow instrument wedges the airlock forever**
§5.1 makes `SeatedInstrument.ready` the trigger for the teardown clock, and §5.1's airlock withholds `pageMotionPermitted` until teardown completes — but no clause bounds how long `ready` may take, and no behavior is defined if `mount()` throws or `ready` never resolves (the spec's own Open Question 1 admits this). A slow instrument therefore delays teardown indefinitely: WorldStage stays mounted, page motion stays forbidden, and G-SH3-1's dormancy never begins — the ten-hour user sits in a permanently half-landed room. I demand a hard deadline on `ready` (e.g., teardown proceeds at N ms regardless), a specified mount-failure path (page-card vignette + route-log row, as the author himself proposes), and a rubric line that kills the shell if a hung instrument can hold the airlock.

**F-2 · HIGH · The departure wait references a promise that does not exist**
§5.1's prose says the shell calls `snapToEnd()` and "waits ≤120ms (or the instrument's resolve, whichever first)" — but `snapToEnd(): void` returns nothing; there is no resolvable thing in the interface. This ambiguity lives in the hottest input path in the system: the departure seam, where SH1's sealed law promises input sovereignty in ≤120ms. An implementer will either always burn the full 120ms (a flat tax on every navigation issued mid-page-motion) or invent an unspecified signal. I demand the interface return a completion signal (`snapToEnd(): Promise<void>` or a callback) and the prose match the type.

**F-3 · HIGH · Preflight decoders contradict the dormancy gate, and abandoned preflight has no teardown deadline**
§2.2's preflight (hover ≥150ms) opens a clip "to first-frame-ready" — a decoder allocation — while the user is seated. G-SH3-1 and §9.1 assert *zero* decoder handles during 10 minutes of seated work "including ≥30 hovers," but the rig never specifies hover durations, so the gate is either unpassable (hovers ≥150ms allocate decoders) or meaningless (all hovers scripted <150ms, and real hesitating hovers go unmeasured). Worse, SH3 nowhere states when an abandoned preflight (hover, then return to typing) must release its decoder — SH1 §4.3's 60s-unwarmed drop is not restated, meaning a decoder can legally sit resident for a minute under a typing hand. I demand: the rig define hover durations straddling 150ms; a hard preflight-abandonment teardown deadline (release on hover-exit + small grace, not 60s); and an explicit statement of whether preflight allocations count against the zero-decoder assertion.

**F-4 · HIGH · G-SH3-2 silently narrows sealed G-SH1-1 to tier-3 only**
Sealed G-SH1-1 gates input-inclusive TTFI for the world layer ON vs hard-cut control across the whole population (p50 may not exceed control). SH3 §1's G-SH3-2 gates "tier-3/drift-cut vs a hard-cut control" only — the first weeks of every route (tiers 0–2, exactly when flights are longest and the user least invested) are ungated in the shipped shell's own launch table. Rubric item 14's "re-run SH1 rubric" does not cover it: G-SH1-1 is a gate, not a rubric item. This is SH3 being weaker than sealed law, the precise crime I'm empaneled for. I demand G-SH3-2 carry the full G-SH1-1 population measure (all tiers, beta cohort) in addition to the tier-3 slice.

**F-5 · HIGH · The route log writes on every transit with no requirement it stays off the critical path**
§4.4 puts SH1 §2.8's schema "verbatim in `studio.sqlite`" via the Rust host, written per navigation — but nothing in §2.4, §4.3, or §4.4 requires the write be asynchronous, batched, or excluded from the queued→seated path. A synchronous IPC-plus-sqlite write per transit is the measurement instrument taxing the thing it measures, invisibly, on all ~hundreds of daily navigations of a resident-tier professional. I demand a written law: route-log persistence is fire-and-forget off the navigation path, never blocks landing or input delivery, with a main-thread budget, and the TTFI protocol (§9.2) must run with logging enabled so the tax cannot hide.

**F-6 · HIGH · The mid-flight reroute leaves an orphaned instrument the lifecycle never enumerates**
§4.3 mounts the destination concurrently with `queued`; SH1 §2.4 (implemented here) says navigating mid-flight "cancels and re-routes." But §5.1 says `mount()` is called "exactly once per landing," and no state, transition, or priority is defined for the already-mounting instrument of a cancelled route: who calls its `unmount()`, when, and whether that teardown may steal frames from the new destination's mount and the ≤120ms skip guarantee. A user who changes their mind mid-flight — the tired user's signature move — hits an unspecified seam. I demand a `cancelled` disposition in the contract (mount-then-cancel ⇒ unmount at idle priority, never contending with the new landing) and a rubric line that mashes reroutes ×50.

**F-7 · HIGH · The pipeline makes the 1am curator do machine work by hand**
§11.5's verify step runs seam checks and encode only; the machine-checkable SH2 acceptance checks — A-2 highlight ceiling, A-4 warm band histogram, A-5 eye-height geometry, A-6 text/figure detector — are left for the canon holder's manual HARVEST-INTAKE across ~58+ assets. Every failed histogram Marcus discovers by eye at 1am is a failure a script should have caught before the asset ever reached the queue. Separately, §11.3's halt ("first-class status, not an error") has no notification or status-summary surface — a detached run can halt silently and waste a night. I demand the pipeline pre-run every machine-measurable A-check at the verify step (attaching results to the intake-queue entry so human attention is spent only on human judgments), and a single `status` command that surfaces halts, failures, and credit state at a glance.

**F-8 · MEDIUM · G-SH3-4's clocks are undefined — the exact defect SH1's Gate 1 already convicted once**
G-SH3-4 asserts "80ms folio paint · ≤2s cold resume · <100ms palette" with no start/stop events named in SH3 (input event? first painted frame? interactive?). SH1's own Gate 1 (F-2) established that an undefined clock produces an instrument that cannot see the harm it exists to catch, and SH1 §4.2 took care to specify G-SH1-6's start line "so the profiler's clock is not arguable." SH3 must extend the same discipline: define each budget's clock (e.g., palette = keydown → first painted, focused input) in §9.4 or the gates are negotiable at measurement time.

**F-9 · MEDIUM · Nothing forbids a flight from waiting on the accretion compositor**
§8 caches composites by `(worldId, accretionHash)`; a `binding.sealed` invalidates the hash, and the very next navigation needs the new composite. No rule states what happens if the worker hasn't finished: the only fatigue-safe answer — serve the stale or base still immediately, swap on next travel, never block — is unstated, so an implementer may legitimately await the composite and add a variable stall to the first post-seal navigation. I demand a written non-blocking law: travel never waits on accretion; staleness is always preferred to delay.

**F-10 · MEDIUM · The UNCURATED watermark's behavior is underspecified where it matters**
§3.2/G-SH3-5 mandate a "small, honest, fixed-corner watermark" whenever an UNCURATED asset is on screen, but never state: that it is static (no pulse, no fade-in animation — during dev a tier-3 drift-cut would otherwise flash it over a live, focused page 240ms at a time, hundreds of times a day); which corner and that it renders only over world-layer pixels, never over the seated instrument; that the runtime check is zero-cost when the manifest is clean or the build is non-dev; and whether curation propagates through composites (an UNCURATED accretion sprite inside a PASS still — §8 — must still trigger it, or the law leaks). The operator running SH3-α lives in dev mode for months; specify all four.

**F-11 · MEDIUM · G-SH3-6's zero-code swap has no reload semantics — the operator relaunches the shell per asset**
§3.3 parses the manifest once at first world-layer initialization; nothing defines hot-reload. The "demonstrated live" swap (G-SH3-6, rubric 4) is undefined without it, and the practical cost lands on the curating founder: one full app relaunch per promoted asset, ~58 times minimum. I demand dev-mode manifest re-read (file-watch or explicit reload command) specified as part of the swap property, so the gate's demonstration and the operator's nights both have a defined mechanism.

**F-12 · MEDIUM · The dormancy gate hangs on a machine that doesn't exist in the spec**
G-SH3-1 is "CI-asserted on the reference machine per release," and Open Question 2 admits the machine class is unnamed, making the assertion aspirational by the spec's own confession. A launch-failing gate whose executor is unspecified is a gate that will be waived under schedule pressure — precisely when my client gets hurt. Name the machine class (CPU/GPU/OS floor) in the spec body and state what happens to releases when the runner is down (block, not skip).

**F-13 · MEDIUM · Ten minutes and a thousand navigations is not ten hours**
§9.1's dormancy rig runs 10 minutes; §9.5's soak is 1,000 back-to-back navigations with a per-seat baseline check. Neither resembles my client's actual day: hours seated with intermittent navigation, the still-LRU, worker, sqlite, and object-URL lifecycle cycling thousands of times across a session. Slow leaks (an unrevoked URL per hundred transits, worker heap creep) are invisible at these durations. I demand one long-form soak — a scripted multi-hour mixed session (seated work + periodic navigation + hovers) with memory and handle counts sampled throughout, asserted flat.

**F-14 · LOW · The 80ms preflight main-thread budget is never required to be sliced**
§2.2 permits "≤80ms main-thread total" per preflight while also citing ≤4ms per intent event, and §9.3 tests only the per-event figure. An implementation that spends its 80ms as one contiguous task drops five frames under a typing hand and still passes §9.3. State that all preflight main-thread work is sliced into ≤4ms tasks (the 80ms is a sum, never a block), and have the trace assertion check longest-task, not just per-event totals.

**F-15 · LOW · Background hash verification and the Class-B probe lack scheduling law**
§3.3's "background sweep at idle" over a ~350MB asset tree has no yield-to-input requirement (teardown got one explicitly in §2.2; the sweep should inherit it verbatim), and §6's 500ms Class-B decode probe "at first run" is never scheduled relative to the ≤2s cold-launch budget — run on the critical path it eats a quarter of it. Both need one sentence each: sweep yields to input; probe runs post-interactivity only.

### Summary

**PATCH.** This is a genuinely fatigue-literate draft — the stills-floor law, the non-blocking drift-cut, keystroke replay, and the no-error-theater fallbacks are exactly what the hundredth day needs, and SH3 mostly transmits sealed law faithfully. But it cannot seal as written, because its weakest points sit precisely on the hottest paths: I would die on **F-1** (an unbounded `ready` promise lets any slow instrument wedge the airlock and forbid page motion indefinitely — the one hole that can make hour ten *worse* than hour one), **F-3** (the preflight/dormancy contradiction means the bench-silence gate is either unpassable or blind, and an abandoned hover can park a decoder under a typing hand), and **F-4** (silently narrowing the sealed population-level TTFI gate to tier-3 leaves the user's entire first month ungated in the shell's own launch table). Fix those three and the rest of the docket, and this spec deserves its seal.

## REVIEWER 2 — THE CANON PROSECUTOR (verbatim)

### Findings

**C-1 · CRITICAL · The ceremony map has vanished: no Rite state, no Rite assets, no Rite stage.** SH1 §4.1 (sealed) mounts `<WorldStage>` "ONLY in flying/**rite** states," and SH1 §3 seals five crossed beats and two bound Rites (the Waking's garth-brightening, the Binding's exhale past the Chronicle shelf, Closing the Volume's pull-back). SH3 §2.2's lifecycle is `dormant → preflight → flight → seated` with no rite state; §3.2 law 3 closes `clipId` to `EXIT|ENTER|ARC|SPOKE|APPROACH`, so the manifest **cannot express** a single Rite or crossed-beat asset; and §10.4's staging (α/β/γ) silently drops SH1 §6.4's **SH1-δ: the Rites**. A sealed subsystem is unimplementable under this schema, and the omission was never raised as an ADR. Remedy: add the rite lifecycle state, a `rites`/`beats` manifest section keyed to SH1 §3.2's closed enumeration, and a δ stage — or an explicit, logged ADR deferring Rites with schema headroom reserved.

**C-2 · HIGH · The manifest is duration-blind: the constitutional ceilings are enforced nowhere, and the spec's own example breaches them.** Canon clause 8: "No Passage exceeds 2,200ms… no build flag, setting, **or asset** may exceed them"; SH1 §2.2 rates `ARC` at ~0.5s and §2.3 shows the worst route at 2.5s raw clip. SH3 §3.1's clip schema carries no duration field, §3.4's CI lint never checks composed-route duration against the ceiling — and §11.2's own exemplar shot record gives `ARC(3,4)` `"duration": 5` while its comment claims "per SH1 §2.2 durations at 1.0×." A 5s arc composes routes far past 2,200ms and nothing in SH3 would catch it before rubric time. Remedy: add `durationMs` to the clip schema, a lint rule composing every route against the ceiling, and correct SR-0031 to ~0.5s (or document a generate-long/trim-at-encode step).

**C-3 · HIGH · The seam-anchor law contradicts itself and Marcus's R4.** R4 (ADR-LOG 2026-07-16) keeps "connectors from **actual rendered neighbor frames**," and SH3 records it twice: §0.2 ("never the source still — 'every generation renders slightly differently'") and §11.8's founding lock "endpoint frames from renders, never stills." Yet §11.4 Law 2 and §11.6 command the opposite: "Frame one of every clip is a banked, intake-PASSed **still**… the pipeline refuses to generate any clip whose `fromPose`/`toPose` **stills** are not PASS." Both cannot be universally true; the pipeline's most load-bearing rule is stated in two irreconcilable forms. Remedy: state the reconciliation explicitly (e.g., pose stills anchor the *first* generation; chained clips anchor to rendered neighbor frames; the ΔE gate verifies both against the locked pose) and rewrite Law 2 and §11.6 to match the lock.

**C-4 · HIGH · The world tint is unimplemented and inexpressible.** SH1 §5.4 (sealed) requires each world's deterministic light-temperature grade; SH2 §5.1 seals "tint applied in post-grade… one neutral master set," and SH2 A-2/A-4 measure "each of the six graded outputs per still." SH3's manifest (§3.1) holds exactly one still file per pose, its compositor (§2.3) never mentions grading, and no clause says whether grades are shipped files or runtime transforms. The engine spec cannot render the sealed per-world evening. Remedy: specify the grade mechanism (pre-graded variants in the manifest vs. a runtime LUT), its determinism inputs (ULID + ruling count per SH1 §5.4), and its A-2/A-4 verification point.

**C-5 · HIGH · Accretion sprite sheets have no manifest home, so the UNCURATED law cannot reach them.** SH3 §8 declares "sprite sheets are pipeline assets (§11) with the same intake discipline," and R2 makes curation flags mechanically consequential — but §3.1's schema has only `poses`, `clips`, `approach`. A sprite sheet cannot carry `curation`, a hash row, or a `shotId` backlink; G-SH3-5/6 and rubric 3–4 are structurally blind to an entire asset class the spec itself puts under them. Remedy: add an `accretion` section to the schema (sheet per SH1 §5.3 channel, hash, curation, shotId, slot bindings) and extend the lint.

**C-6 · MEDIUM · Nobody is specified to write PASS — the never-self-ratify law has an unnamed second half.** The seal note in SH2 and R2/R4 ("never self-ratifies past HARVEST-INTAKE") plus G-SH3-8 forbid the pipeline any PASS-writing code path — correct. But §11.5 step 6 says "PASS replaces the curation object" without naming the instrument that performs the write, and §11.2's shot-record schema (pipeline-owned files, per §11.7 freely re-read/re-written for resumability) includes `PASS` in its own status enum. If the CLI is the only tool in the repo, the pipeline *is* the PASS writer and G-SH3-8 is falsified by construction. Remedy: specify the separate intake instrument (or manual procedure) that writes PASS, and partition shot-record fields into pipeline-writable vs. intake-writable.

**C-7 · MEDIUM · `devMode` is undefined, and R2's whole mechanism hangs on it.** R2 (ADR-LOG 2026-07-16): "CI fails any build whose manifest contains one **outside dev mode**." SH3 §3.2 law 2 makes UNCURATED "valid only under `devMode: true` shell config" — but never says whether devMode is a compile-time build variant or a runtime setting. If it is runtime-flippable config, a shipped build can enable it and render UNCURATED assets watermark-and-all, gutting the CI gate. Remedy: define devMode as a compile-time distinction the CI lint and the runtime check can both verify, with no runtime toggle.

**C-8 · MEDIUM · "Closed enum, 9 bays" contradicts SH1's geography.** SH1 §2.1 (sealed) has **eight** bays (I–VIII) plus the Sanctum, which "is the garth" — explicitly not a bay — and names Campaign Studio "the **ninth bay**" as a future schema-version event (SH3 §3.2 law 3 agrees). SH3 §5.1's `BayId // closed enum, 9 bays` either miscounts by absorbing the Sanctum into "bay" (breaking SH1's ontology and leaving the garth-center seat's semantics undefined for `SeatSurface`) or pre-mints Campaign Studio in defiance of its own schema-version law. Remedy: `BayId` = 8 bays; a separate `sanctum` destination (or a 9-value `RoomId` explicitly distinguished from bays), with the garth-center seat's contract stated.

**C-9 · MEDIUM · The schema exemplar puts an anchor slot where sealed framing law says it cannot exist.** SH1 §5.2 places anchor slots in "room's **interior** stills," and SH2 §5.4 (sealed) fixes the lintel pose "facing the garth… **own bay unseen**." SH3 §3.1's example attaches `{"slotId": "chronicle.shelf"}` to `poseId: "lintel.chronicle"` — a Chronicle-shelf slot on a frame that constitutionally cannot show the Chronicle. Exemplars teach; this one teaches a violation. Remedy: move the example slot to the seated/bench pose (whose framing SH2 §5.4 gives "accretion anchors peripheral") and lint slot placement against pose family.

**C-10 · MEDIUM · The throwaway pane's "880ms decorative motion" is unlawful page motion.** Clause 8: the page's four registers govern "every pixel that moves while a page is seated," and GENESIS 03 §VI (per ADR-SH1-B) reserves 880ms for **ceremony** alone. SH3 §5.2 gives the placeholder folio "one registered 880ms **decorative** motion behind a button" — a non-ceremony pixel at the ceremony register, the definitional performing pixel, with no dev-mode exemption written anywhere in sealed law. The airlock test fixture is itself a register violation. Remedy: declare it a simulated Binding seal (ceremony register, named as test fixture) or cap it at 520ms; either way, name the choice.

**C-11 · MEDIUM · G-SH3-1 silently hardens away SH1's preflight carve-out and thereby outlaws SH3's own §2.2.** SH1 G-SH1-6 pairs "zero GPU/decoder allocation" with an explicit carve-out ("preflight main-thread cost ≤4ms per intent event, all decode off-thread"); SH3 §2.2's preflight opens a clip "to first-frame-ready" on hover ≥150ms — decoder allocation. G-SH3-1's threshold column demands flat "zero decoders" during a rig including "≥30 hovers" with no dwell specified and no preflight carve-out: as written, any correct implementation of §2.2 fails the gate. Remedy: restate the carve-out in G-SH3-1's threshold and pin the rig's hover dwell (e.g., hovers straddle the 150ms trigger deliberately, with preflight's budget as the pass criterion).

**C-12 · LOW · The SeatSurface interface cannot express its own prose.** §5.1's prose enforces the airlock "by withholding a `pageMotionPermitted` signal until teardown completes" and the shell "waits ≤120ms (or the instrument's resolve, whichever first)" on departure — but the TS contract has no `pageMotionPermitted` member anywhere and `snapToEnd(): void` returns nothing to resolve. The interface handed to SPEC-002 (R3) omits the two mechanics that make it the airlock's contract; mount-failure is confessed open (doubt #1) but uncured. Remedy: add the permission signal (event/promise on `SeatContext`), make `snapToEnd` return a completion promise, and specify mount-failure → page-card vignette as a contract clause, not a doubt.

**C-13 · LOW · Unnamed numbers and an unnamed machine in launch gates.** SSIM ≥ 0.985 (G-SH3-3) is a fresh legal threshold with no named-choice justification (SH1 seals only ΔE76 < 2.0; the Named-Choice Doctrine binds every such number); G-SH3-1's "GPU attributable-delta **indistinguishable** from control" has no numeric bound; and the reference CI machine is unnamed — confessed at doubt #2, which does not cure a gate the spec calls launch-failing. Remedy: name the 0.985 derivation, replace "indistinguishable" with a bound, name the machine class in the gate row.

**C-14 · LOW · The Shot Record addendum's lettered citations are unauditable against the log.** SH3 cites "addendum (a)…(e)" throughout §11 (and ADR-SH3-B claims "Marcus delegated this call — R4(e)"), but the ADR-LOG's 2026-07-16 R4 entry enumerates no lettered clauses, and item (d) — "submission is programmatic via the Higgsfield MCP" — appears in no logged ruling at all (the log records only Higgsfield *access* restored, ADR-SH2-E). Law you cannot locate is law you cannot verify. Remedy: file the addendum's text verbatim in the ADR-LOG (append-only, per its own header) or re-cite against what the log actually says.

**C-15 · LOW · The `register` pin points at an identifier that does not exist.** §3.1/§3.2 law 5 pins the manifest to `"lanternlight-v1"`, which "must equal SH2 seal id" — but sealed SH2 mints no seal-id string anywhere; the pin is unenforceable until the identifier is minted somewhere authoritative. Remedy: mint the id in the ADR-LOG (or an SH2 companion note) so the lint has a source of truth.

### Summary

**REWORK.** The draft is genuinely faithful in its skeleton — jurisdiction over `packages/core` and SPEC-002 is clean (interface proposals only, per R3), the UNCURATED mechanics honor R2, and the airlock, decay, and dormancy laws are mostly transcribed with care — but its central artifact, the manifest schema, is convicted three ways: it cannot express the sealed ceremony map at all (C-1, a whole SH1 subsystem silently dropped from lifecycle, enum, and staging), it is blind to the constitutional duration ceilings while the spec's own exemplar shot record breaches them tenfold (C-2), and it cannot carry the accretion assets or tint grades the spec itself places under its law (C-4, C-5). Gravest alongside these is C-3, where the pipeline's seam-anchor rule is written in two mutually exclusive forms, one of which contradicts Marcus's recorded R4. These are schema-and-law failures, not polish notes: the manifest is "the single source of world truth," and a source of truth that cannot express required truths — and can silently admit forbidden ones — must be reopened before this document may face a seal.

## REVIEWER 3 — THE TASTE AUDITOR (verbatim)

### Findings

**T-1 · CRITICAL · The night bird and the footsteps (§11.4, AUDIO)**
The template's AUDIO line reads "none, or near-silence — faint footsteps on flagstone at walking rhythm, distant single night bird, nothing else." SH1 §2.7 is absolute: travel is silent, always. The note's defense — audio direction exists only to steer the model's *motion* — is legitimate mechanism convicting its own content: if the audio channel steers visuals, then footstep rhythm steers toward camera bob (which the CAMERA line explicitly bans) and toward an embodied walker (SH2 ADR-SH2-C: no figures or their traces, ever), and a night bird is an *event* smuggled into a template whose stated job is "to forbid event beats." This is the spec's own poetry exceeding its discipline in the exact section built to prevent that. Replacement demanded, verbatim: `AUDIO: none. The corridor is silent — no footsteps, no wildlife, no wind, no music. Silence is a motion direction: nothing in the world is moving enough to make a sound.`

**T-2 · HIGH · "True darkness" directs the generator to break sealed light law (§11.4, PHYSICS)**
"Pools of amber alternating with true darkness" contradicts SH2 §1.2 ("darkness is generous: deepest shadow at obsidian #141310, never pure black") and the 1983 clause's "lifted soft blacks." A prompt is legislation aimed at a model; this sentence legislates a violation that A-4 will then reject at cost. Replacement demanded: `light falloff obeys inverse square — pools of amber alternating with deep warm shadow, lifted soft blacks, never pure black.`

**T-3 · HIGH · The UNCURATED watermark is honest evidence with an unauthored body (§3.2, G-SH3-5)**
The watermark itself is the right instinct — honest scaffolding, the opposite of a performing pixel. But "a small, honest, fixed-corner watermark" specifies nothing: no typeface, no corner, no material, no size — "the model did it" in prose form, and it is *text painted over world frames*, which SH2 §1.1 bans outright with no dev-mode exception written anywhere. An honest mark whose design is left to chance will be designed by whoever implements it, which is how template-stock enters a canon. Demand: name the exception and the body in one sentence — `Dev-mode exception, scoped and closed: the UNCURATED mark is a page-material chip in the shell's chrome layer (page jurisdiction, never composited into the world frame), IBM Plex Mono caption size, ink on page ground, fixed bottom-left, static — the page-card vignette's precedent, worn as a tag.`

**T-4 · HIGH · SSIM 0.985 is a launch-failing number nobody chose (G-SH3-3, §3.4)**
SH1's ΔE < 2.0 arrives with a spike measurement (0.11) and a seal; SSIM ≥ 0.985 arrives from nowhere — no derivation, no spike, no sentence, yet it rejects assets in CI. The Named-Choice Doctrine (canon §3; SH2 A-12: "the model did it" fails) governs gate numbers above all, because an unnamed gate number is either untested generosity or untested cruelty. Demand the naming sentence or the number's demotion to provisional: `SSIM ≥ 0.985 because [measured seam pairs from the S2 spike scored ≥ X / the structural-drift failure it exists to catch scores ≤ Y]; provisional until the first ten production seams calibrate it.`

**T-5 · HIGH · The arithmetic SH3 never does: 5 seconds, 1.2 m/s, and a 0.5s arc (§11.2, §11.4)**
The example shot record declares `"duration": 5` with the comment "per SH1 §2.2 durations at 1.0×" — but SH1 §2.2 seals ARC at ~0.5s. At the template's 1.2 m/s, a 0.5s arc traverses 0.6 meters, which crosses no cloister bay; at 5s it traverses 6m, which is a different geography. SH1 modeled the discipline exactly (§2.3's "ceiling arithmetic" paragraph); SH3 skipped it, and Open Question 3 confesses only half the tension. Demand the missing paragraph: whether clips are generated long and trimmed, or the durations are per-clip law, stated with the meters-per-bay arithmetic shown — and the shot-record comment corrected or the example convicted.

**T-6 · MEDIUM · A test fixture granted "decorative" motion in the ceremony register (§5.2)**
The placeholder folio carries "one registered 880ms decorative motion behind a button." Its purpose is sound — proving `snapToEnd` at the page's longest legal duration — but the spec names it with the one word canon exists to kill ("a pixel that merely performs is removed, regardless of its beauty"), leaves its visual unspecified, and hands the ceremony register (880ms is the *Binding's* duration class) to a throwaway. Fixtures inherit taste law or they teach the codebase that dev builds are lawless. Replacement demanded: `one 880ms airlock-probe motion (a flat token-grey bar sweep, deliberately inert — it exists to be interrupted, not watched), exercising snapToEnd at the page's longest legal duration; the word "decorative" does not exist in this repo.`

**T-7 · MEDIUM · The template hard-codes 165cm against SH2's three sealed eye heights (§11.4, FIRST FRAME)**
SH2 §1.1 seals 1.55m at bench poses, 1.60m at the shelf, 1.65m at lintels and garth. The call-sheet's FIRST FRAME fixes "eye height 165cm" — correct for ARCs, wrong for every EXIT/ENTER clip, which begins or ends at a 1.55m bench pose. A template that contradicts sealed geometry for half the clip grammar is stock thinking wearing the register's clothes. Demand parameterization: `FIRST FRAME: exactly the start reference — eye height {POSE_EYE_HEIGHT per SH2 §5.4}, level, facing [bearing].`

**T-8 · MEDIUM · The world notices you passing (§11.4, ACTION)**
"Flame-light flickers faintly in each lantern *as it passes*" makes flame behavior causally keyed to the traveler — the world responding to your presence, which is the seed of every performing pixel and the precise thing the lodge law killed one document upstream. The PHYSICS line already has the correct account (sway from still air). Replacement demanded: `flame-light flickers faintly and indifferently — the flames were moving before the walker arrived and continue after; nothing in the world responds to the walker's passage.`
