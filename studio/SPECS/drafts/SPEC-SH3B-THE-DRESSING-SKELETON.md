## The three riskiest open questions
1. **How far does the navigation object go before it becomes the Atlas's cousin?** A brass cloister-ring device that shows the nine rooms *as geography* is the obvious authored answer — and it edges toward "the workshop as spatial canvas," which GENESIS 09-II killed and ADR-SH1-A fenced. The fence held because SH1 spatializes *travel*, not data. A nav device rendering the ring is arguably the same lawful ground (nine fixed rooms, no user content) — but it renders geography *while seated*, on the page layer, permanently visible. Does the bench-silence spirit (world exists as information during work) permit a persistent geographic device in chrome, or must navigation stay typographic (spine-tabs) with geography only felt in travel? My lean: the device is lawful as *page-material iconography* (thin-stroke, Ledger register — ADR-SH2-D's own boundary) and unlawful as *world rendering*; the distinction needs to be written before a designer blurs it.
2. **Where does SH3-β's authority end and the design-production campaign's begin?** The ladder defers `@ash-archive/ledger-ui`'s exhaustive render specs to a final campaign; CB1 §3 holds a "minimum true bench" line with PROVISIONAL headers. SH3-β now authors real chrome. Risk: β quietly *becomes* the design system nobody sealed — the exact self-deception CB1's Gate 1 (T-3) convicted once already. Proposed line: β owns **shell chrome only** (rail-replacement, thresholds, degraded cards, seat container's outer frame); everything inside the folio's content box stays CB1/design-production territory; β's tokens are *contributed to* the future ledger-ui sheet, not minted as a parallel one. Is that line enforceable by lint, or only by review?
3. **Can the five-second bar be gated without becoming taste theater?** "A stranger knows in five seconds" is the right bar and an unfalsifiable gate as stated. Options: (a) the SH2 A-9 pattern — cold-viewer panel, structured questions ("have you seen this app before?" / "name the material this is made of"), N named viewers, pass thresholds; (b) the taste auditor's veto stands in for it at the gate, and the bar stays aspirational prose; (c) both — panel at seal, veto at gate. My lean: (c), with the panel's questions fixed in the spec now so the test can't be softened after the fact. The risk worth naming: a panel we recruit is a panel we can unconsciously coach; the question set must be written blind to the designs.
