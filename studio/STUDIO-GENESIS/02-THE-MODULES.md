# 02 — THE MODULES
### The nine rooms of the Studio

Every module is a **Wing** under the ecosystem canon: same Entry graph, same Ash/Archive duality, same Ledger System, same six-verb grammar, same Dramaturg constitution. A module is a *room*, not a silo — kill any one and the others still stand; use them together and the loop closes. Each is specified as: purpose · problem solved · features · dependencies · priority · monetization value.

---

## 1. THE CODEX — the Player Academy & table instrument *(flagship; fully specified)*

- **Purpose:** the player's and DM's live instrument — character forging, the self-turning Table, Masks, the Binding, personal growth record.
- **Problem:** play is where worlds live and where every other tool goes dark.
- **Features:** complete in `products/the-codex/GENESIS/` (v2.0, council-hardened). Nothing here overrides it.
- **Dependencies:** the Foundation (Entry graph + Ash) only.
- **Priority:** P0 — it is the proof of the substrate and the mobile beachhead.
- **Monetization:** the Ember tier *is* the Codex; it is also every player's on-ramp into a GM's world (network growth vector).

## 2. THE WORLD FORGE — worldbuilding as engineering *(the Studio's center of gravity)*

- **Purpose:** build worlds that *run*, not encyclopedias that sit. The founder's world-substrate methodology, made into instruments: **Gravity Truths** (3–7 immutable laws), the **Power Lattice** (≥5 actors with goals, constraints, enforcement), the **Constraints Map** (travel friction, chokepoints, scarcity), faith/magic social contracts with **discernment tells**, **Toys** (the modular parts bin), **Pressure Clocks**, **Portable Truths** (secrets with levers and ≥3 delivery vectors), and bounded **UNKNOWNs**.
- **Problem:** every worldbuilding tool measures completeness by article count; worlds stall as lore accumulates without handles. The Forge measures **readiness to generate play**.
- **Features:** guided substrate folios per methodology template · the Toybox at world scale · civilization/faction/religion/economy frames as typed Entries with consequence links · the **Relationship Web** (the consequence graph rendered — force-directed views of *threatens/serves/hides/unlocks*) · timelines & eras · the world-scale **Readiness Gate** as the Forge's signature instrument (domains filling toward PASS; the Dramaturg refuses to scaffold campaigns on an un-PASSed world).
- **Dependencies:** Foundation; Charter Room (status/rulings).
- **Priority:** P0 — this is what "Studio" means beyond the Codex.
- **Monetization:** the Forge-tier anchor. Unique claim on the market's #1 unmet need (canon-governed, play-ready worldbuilding).

## 3. THE CHARTER ROOM — canon governance *(the moat, studio-wide)*

- **Purpose:** the court of what is true. Canon Ledger views across the four layers (immutable → structural → dynamic → local), the **Contradiction Bench** (detect → classify → three patch options → lock), alias protocol, patch history, the Readiness Gate's machinery.
- **Problem:** canon drift is invisible in every existing tool until it breaks trust; no competitor even has the concept.
- **Features:** as specified in Codex GENESIS 05-IV, promoted to studio scope: contradiction detection runs across *all* modules' writes; every Binding, Forge edit, and Press publication is checked; multiplayer conflict arbitration lands here in v2.0.
- **Dependencies:** Foundation.
- **Priority:** P0 (ships with the Forge; it is the Forge's spine).
- **Monetization:** not sold separately — it is *why* professionals trust the platform with a decade of work.

## 4. CAMPAIGN STUDIO — narrative architecture

- **Purpose:** the arc above the session: story spines, acts and beats, plot-thread management (active/dormant/resolved with dependencies), branching timelines, session planning with pacing awareness, downtime and faction turns between sessions.
- **Problem:** campaign structure lives in DMs' heads and dies in prep docs; no tool connects the arc to what actually happened at the table.
- **Features:** thread board over Scene/Session/Clock Entries · the Eight-Step prep worksheet (from the Codex, deepened) · arc-level clocks and off-screen motion planning · "Previously" generation · chronicle-aware planning (the plan sees what the table actually did, because the Binding recorded it).
- **Dependencies:** Foundation; World Forge (the world it happens in); Chronicle.
- **Priority:** P1.
- **Monetization:** Forge-tier; the serious GM's weekly home.

## 5. THE STAGE — the DM's live table & performance layer

- **Purpose:** the DM-side Table at studio power: the Loop spread (from Codex GENESIS 04-IV), encounter running (Cohorts, interrupt layer, statblocks), the Hidden folio, kindling from the Toybox — plus, at Atelier tier, **performance mode**: a spectator-facing composed view, streamer overlays in the Ledger System's register, session recording marks, subscriber-visible lore reveals.
- **Problem:** VTTs render maps but know nothing of the world; nothing on the market persists table events into lore; streamers assemble presentation from four tools.
- **Features:** the Codex's DM Table (P0, ships with Codex) · encounter architect instruments (P1) · performance mode + revenue integrations (P2/Atelier). Explicitly *not* a VTT: no token-and-grid simulation in v1 — the Stage complements a physical table or an existing VTT; a map-view instrument may come later as a view over Place Entries.
- **Dependencies:** Codex Table machinery; World Forge for kindling.
- **Priority:** P0 core (inside Codex) / P2 performance mode.
- **Monetization:** performance mode is the Atelier tier's headline — the unmet streamer need with direct revenue attached.

## 6. THE CHRONICLE & THE LEDGER — the world's record

- **Purpose:** the Binding, the bound chapters, the Growth Record, session metrics — studio-wide (from Codex GENESIS 06, promoted): *every* module's activity flows through ash and can be bound.
- **Problem:** "what happened in session 4" and "what changed in my world this month" are unanswerable everywhere else.
- **Features:** as specified in Codex GENESIS 06 + world-scale views (World Pulse — the recovered dashboard panel — becomes real: the activity feed is the ash, rendered).
- **Dependencies:** Foundation.
- **Priority:** P0/P1 (with Codex phase 2).
- **Monetization:** retention engine — the record of years of play is the deepest switching cost honestly earned (and always exportable, per the covenant).

## 7. THE ACADEMY — the school *(threading every module)*

- **Purpose:** the transformation ladder: prescription engine over Ledger evidence, the drill lines, curriculum shelf (the founder's doctrine as course texts), Warmups, the rehearsal room — per Codex GENESIS 06 Part Two, extended with Forge-craft (worldbuilding drills: Toy quality, truth-lever craft) and campaign-craft curricula.
- **Problem:** no creative tool teaches; the Studio's true product is the user's mastery.
- **Features:** as specified; Path → Module → Lesson → Drill structure from the recovered Pillar 3 architecture, rebuilt on prescriptions-from-evidence rather than content browsing.
- **Dependencies:** Chronicle (evidence); Dramaturg (Coach voice).
- **Priority:** P1.
- **Monetization:** the differentiator no competitor can bolt on; drives the upgrade from Ember to Forge ("the Academy read my session").

## 8. THE DRAMATURG — the studio's AI *(one constitution, every room)*

- **Purpose:** the five voices (Ideator, Builder, Archivist, Coach, Co-DM) under the constitution (Codex GENESIS 07) — at studio scale: Builder drafts in every Forge template, Archivist groups every module's ash and guards every Binding, Ideator sharpens campaign arcs, Coach runs the Academy.
- **Problem:** every "AI-powered" competitor is a hallucination engine with a chat sidebar. The Dramaturg is governed: pencil-only, provenance-marked, canon-cited, gate-bound, chat-window-free.
- **Features:** as constitutionally specified; studio additions: cross-module staging (the same Entry subgraph feeds every voice), model routing per voice latency class, prompts-as-data in the Charter Room, BYO-model and metered-cloud options.
- **Dependencies:** Foundation; Charter Room (the Gate binds it).
- **Priority:** P1 (after the silent instruments are excellent — the Codex discipline holds studio-wide).
- **Monetization:** metered/priority AI is the honest cost-recovery line in Forge/Archive tiers; the *governance* is the sales argument.

## 9. THE PRESS — publishing & the creator pipeline

- **Purpose:** the world leaves the Studio as artifacts: chronicles composed into books (PDF/print-ready/EPUB), subscriber **lore portals** (public, reader-aware views over LOCKED Entries), Patreon-style drip-feeds (schedule Entry reveals to subscribers), campaign-setting exports, and the ownership covenant's beautiful face (print folios of characters and chapters).
- **Problem:** market gap #6 verbatim: no bridge exists between worldbuilding and publishing/monetization; creators curate manually across four products.
- **Features:** composition templates in the Ledger System's typography · reveal scheduling bound to canon status (only LOCKED publishes; PROVISIONAL/UNKNOWN structurally cannot leak) · reader-knowledge tracking ("what have my patrons seen") · web portal hosting (the one place a server enters the architecture — portals are published *out*, canon never lives *on* the server).
- **Dependencies:** Chronicle; Charter Room (publication is a canon act).
- **Priority:** P2 — but designed-for from P0 (the export covenant is its embryo).
- **Monetization:** the Archive/Atelier tiers' engine; converts the Studio from a cost into the creator's storefront.
